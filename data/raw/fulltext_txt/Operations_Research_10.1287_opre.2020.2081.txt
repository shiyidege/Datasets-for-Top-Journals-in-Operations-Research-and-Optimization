Abstract
A Ô¨āuid queuing network constitutes one of the simplest models in
which to study Ô¨āow dynamics over a network. In this model we have a
single source-sink pair and each link has a per-time-unit capacity and
a transit time. A dynamic equilibrium (or equilibrium Ô¨āow over time)
is a Ô¨āow pattern over time such that no Ô¨āow particle has incentives to
unilaterally change its path. Although the model has been around for
almost Ô¨Āfty years, only recently results regarding existence and charac-
terization of equilibria have been obtained. In particular the long term
behavior remains poorly understood. Our main result in this paper
is to show that, under a natural (and obviously necessary) condition
on the queuing capacity, a dynamic equilibrium reaches a steady state
(after which queue lengths remain constant) in Ô¨Ānite time. Previously,
it was not even known that queue lengths would remain bounded. The
proof is based on the analysis of a rather non-obvious potential function
that turns out to be monotone along the evolution of the equilibrium.
Furthermore, we show that the steady state is characterized as an op-
timal solution of a certain linear program. When this program has
a unique solution, which occurs generically, the long term behavior
is completely predictable. On the contrary, if the linear program has
multiple solutions the steady state is more diÔ¨Écult to identify as it
depends on the whole temporal evolution of the equilibrium.

from a lack of coordination among the participating agents, it is natural to

congestion and queuing delays induced by others upon arrival to any edge in

empty, the equilibrium should start by sending all the Ô¨āow along shortest

Conjectures on more general steady state results
Suppose that the inÔ¨āow into the network is not a constant, but a time varying
function u0(őł). Suppose moreover that u0(őł) is always bounded by the min-
cut capacity of the network.
Of course, there cannot be convergence to

argument can aid in proving this conjecture. In particular, note that the
