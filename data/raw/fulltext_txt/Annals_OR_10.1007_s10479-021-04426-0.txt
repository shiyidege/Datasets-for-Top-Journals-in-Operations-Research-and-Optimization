Abstract
The robust minimum cost ’¼éow problem under consistent ’¼éow constraints (RobMCFŌēĪ) is
a new extension of the minimum cost ’¼éow (MCF) problem. In the RobMCFŌēĪproblem,
we consider demand and supply that are subject to uncertainty. For all demand realizations,
however, we require that the ’¼éow value on an arc needs to be equal if it is included in the
predetermined arc set given. The objective is to ’¼ünd feasible ’¼éows that satisfy the equal ’¼éow
requirements while minimizing the maximum occurring cost among all demand realizations.
In the case of a ’¼ünite discrete set of scenarios, we derive structural results which point out
the differences with the polynomial time solvable MCF problem in networks with integral
demands, supplies, and capacities. In particular, the Integral Flow Theorem of Dantzig and
Fulkerson does not hold. For this reason, we require integral ’¼éows in the entire paper. We
show that the RobMCFŌēĪproblem is strongly NP-hard on acyclic digraphs by a reduction
from the (3, B2)-Sat problem. Further, we demonstrate that the RobMCFŌēĪproblem is
weakly NP-hard on series-parallel digraphs by providing a reduction from Partition. If
in addition the number of scenarios is constant, we propose a pseudo-polynomial algorithm
based on dynamic programming. Finally, we present a special case on series-parallel digraphs
for which we can solve the RobMCFŌēĪproblem in polynomial time.

be identi’¼üed by a ’¼éow sent through a network from a supply source to a sink with demand.
In this way, a company can easily assess whether the available means of transport are suf’¼ü-

be arranged. Such arrangements are generally agreed by long-term contracts, however, the
demand is naturally subject to uncertainty. For this reason, valid and cost-ef’¼ücient decisions
have to be made without the knowledge of the actual demand.
The problem described can be represented by an adjusted integral MCF model subject to
demand uncertainty. We represent the demand uncertainty by a discrete number of possible
occurring demand scenarios. In addition to the network requirements of the MCF problem,
we are given a predetermined set of arcs referred to as ’¼üxed arcs. The ’¼éow value on the ’¼üxed
arcs is supposed to represent the transport by subcontractors. Thus, we require the ’¼éow value
on a ’¼üxed arc to be equal among all demand scenarios. For ’¼ünding a robust solution to this
problem, we minimize the maximum cost that may occur among all demand scenarios. In
summary, for the RobMCFŌēĪproblem, we consider different demand scenarios for which
we require integral ’¼éows whose ’¼éow values are equal on the respective ’¼üxed arcs with the
objective of minimizing the maximum cost.
The main contribution of this paper can be summarized as follows. We show that most of
the knowledge of the MCF problem is not readily transferable to the RobMCFŌēĪproblem. In
particular, the integrality requirement of the RobMCFŌēĪproblem is necessary, even though
the demands and supplies of all demand scenarios and the networkŌĆÖs capacities are integral,
as Dantzig and FulkersonŌĆÖs Integral Flow Theorem (Korte et al. 2012) does not hold. We
further prove that ’¼ünding a feasible solution to the RobMCFŌēĪproblem is strongly NP-
complete on acyclic digraphs, even if only two demand scenarios and unit arc capacities are
considered. On series-parallel digraphs, we show that the decision version of the RobMCFŌēĪ
problem is weakly NP-complete and in the special case of a constant number of scenarios
it is solvable in pseudo-polynomial time by dynamic programming. If all demand scenarios
have the same single source and sink, we propose an algorithm running in polynomial time
on series-parallel digraphs.

2 Related work
In the context of logistic applications, several extensions to the MCF problem are analyzed
in the literature. We refer to the minimum cost ’¼éows with minimum quantities problem intro-
duced by Seedig (2011) and generalized by Krumke and Thielen (2011). In addition to the
requirements of the MCF problem, on each arc the ’¼éow must either take the value zero or a
minimum quantity given. This can be altered and transfered to the case where the ’¼éow on the
’¼üxed arcs, which represents the transport by subcontractors, takes a minimum value (among
all demand scenarios). Another extension is obtained, for example, by the MCF version of the
maximum ’¼éow problem with disjunctive constraints studied by Pferschy and Schauer (2013).

Sahni (1974) introduces a variant of the maximum ’¼éow (MF) problem (Ahuja et al. 1988),

predetermined arc set. Sahni proves the NP-hardness of the problem by a reduction from
the Non- Tautology problem. Johnson and Garey (1979) point out that by modifying a
construction of Even et al. (1975), the problemŌĆÖs NP-hardness holds even if all arc capacities
are equal to one. Furthermore, unless P = NP, the non-existence of a 2n(1ŌłÆ╬Ą)-approximation
algorithm for any ’¼üxed ╬Ą > 0 (on a digraph with n vertices) is proven by Meyers and Schulz
(2009) even if a nonzero solution is guaranteed to exist.

Meyers and Schulz 2009; Morrison et al. 2013; Srinathan et al. 2002). For instance, the special
case of the EF problem where all sets have cardinality two, i.e., an integral MCF is sought
whose ’¼éow value is equal on a predetermined set of arc pairs, is investigated by Ali et al.
(1988). The problem ’¼ünds application in, for example, crew scheduling (Carraresi and Gallo
1984). Therefore, Ali et al. present a heuristic algorithm based on Lagrangian relaxation.
Meyers and Schulz (2009) refer to this problem as paired integer equal ’¼éow problem (pEF)
and prove that there exists no 2n(1ŌłÆ╬Ą)-approximation algorithm for any ’¼üxed ╬Ą > 0 (on a
digraph with n vertices), unless P = NP. The statement holds true even if a nontrivial
solution is guaranteed to exist. A simpler and in polynomial time solvable special case of the
EF problem is the so-called simple equal ’¼éow problem (sEF), which is introduced by Ahuja
et al. (1999). The sEF problem requires the same but not necessarily integral ’¼éow value on
only a single predetermined set of arcs. The problem is motivated by the management of
water resource systems (Manca et al. 2010). For this purpose, Ahuja et al. (1999) develop
several ef’¼ücient algorithms to solve large-scale instancesŌĆöa network simplex, a parametric
simplex, a combinatorial parametric, a binary search, and a capacity scaling algorithm. These
algorithms can easily be modi’¼üed to obtain integral solutions.
Unlike the previous research on MF and MCF problems with equal ’¼éow requirements,
we consider in the RobMCFŌēĪproblem not one demand scenario only, but several demand
scenarios. For each of these scenarios, a feasible ’¼éow is sought. Furthermore, among all
of these scenarios, we require the same ’¼éow value on an arc if it is included in the single
predetermined set of ’¼üxed arcs. Although we consider several demand scenarios, the problem
of ’¼ünding a feasible solution to the RobMCFŌēĪproblem can be modeled as a special case
of the EF and pEF problem by means of graph copies. We point out that the equal ’¼éow
requirements in the RobMCFŌēĪproblem are only of importance while considering different
demand scenarios, i.e., the ’¼éow value of a ’¼üxed arc has to be equal among all scenarios.
In turn, the ’¼éow value of two ’¼üxed arcs may differ in one scenario. For this reason, the
problem of ’¼ünding a feasible solution to the RobMCFŌēĪproblem cannot be modeled as the

Demand uncertainty is studied more frequently in the context of network design and
network engineering in telecommunication or road networks for example. In robust network
design, we have to decide on the capacities such that in all considered scenarios, the entire
demand can simultaneously be routed. The cost of installing the capacities is supposed to be
minimized.
In the single commodity case which was ’¼ürst studied by Minoux (1989) and by Sanit├Ā
(2009), the ’¼éow between supply and demand vertices may differ among the scenarios as

discrete set of scenarios, and a polytope. Atamt├╝rk and Zhang (2007) present a two-stage
robust optimization approach where some capacity decisions have to be made before, and
other after the demand realization. The decisions have to guarantee that in any case the
demand can be routed through the network.
In the multi-commodity case, several studies propose different models and uncertainty
sets. For instance, Altin et al. (2007, 2011) propose the so-called Hose uncertainty model,
Belotti et al. (2008) in the context of statistical multiplexing, and Koster et al. (2013) the
budget uncertainty set introduced by Bertsimas and Sim (2003, 2004). In these studies, the
’¼éow is sent proportionally with the demand. In case the ’¼éow can be adapted to the demand,
a two-stage robust approach is followed. While Mattia (2013) studies dynamic routing, Poss
and Raack (2013) suggest to use af’¼üne recourse options.

In this section, we consider the RobMCFŌēĪproblem on series-parallel (SP) digraphs. We

We start with a formal de’¼ünition for SP digraphs based on the edge SP multi-graphs
de’¼ünition of Valdes et al. (1982).
De’¼ünition 4 Series-parallel (SP) digraphs can be recursively de’¼üned as follows.
1. An arc (o, q) is an SP digraph with origin o and target q.
2. Let G1 with origin o1 and target q1 and G2 with origin o2 and target q2 be SP digraphs.
The digraph that is constructed by one of the following two compositions of SP digraphs
G1 and G2 is itself an SP digraph.
(a) The series composition G of two SP digraphs G1 and G2 is the digraph obtained by
contracting target q1 and origin o2. The origin of digraph G is then o1 (becoming o),
and the target is q2 (becoming q).
(b) The parallel composition G of two SP digraphs G1 and G2 is the digraph obtained
by contracting origins o1 and o2 (becoming o) and contracting targets q1 and q2
(becoming q). The new origin of digraph G is o, and the target is q.
The parallel and series compositions are illustrated in Fig. 3a. Note that, by de’¼ünition, SP
digraphs are generally multi-graphs with one de’¼ünite origin and one de’¼ünite target.
A useful property of SP digraphs is their representability in the form of a rooted binary
decomposition tree, a so-called SP tree, visualized in Fig. 3b. For a given SP digraph, we
construct an SP tree that represents the order of the series and parallel compositions of
individual arcs. By means of three different vertices, namely L-vertices, S-vertices, and P-
vertices, single arcs as well as series and parallel compositions are indicated. The SP treeŌĆÖs
leaves are L-vertices where there exist as many L-vertices as the digraph represented has arcs.
The S- and P-vertices are the SP treeŌĆÖs inner vertices and correspond to the digraph obtained
by a series or, respectively, parallel composition of the subgraphs associated with their two
child vertices. The order of the children of P-vertices is irrelevant while it is essential for
S-vertices as the series composition is not commutative. Following the constructions of all

6 Conclusion
In this paper, we introduced the RobMCFŌēĪproblem which is an extension of the MCF prob-
lem considering equal ’¼éow requirements and demand uncertainty. We presented structural
results which differentiate from well known results of the MCF problem. In particular, we
showed that Dantzig and FulkersonŌĆÖs Integral Flow Theorem (Korte et al. 2012) does not hold
anymore. Furthermore, we proved that ’¼ünding a feasible solution to the RobMCFŌēĪproblem
is strongly NP-complete on acyclic digraphs, even if a network with a unique source, a
unique sink, and unit arc capacities are considered for two scenarios only. On SP digraphs,
we proved that the decision version of the RobMCFŌēĪproblem is weakly NP-complete. For
the special case in which the number of scenarios is not part of the input, we proposed a
pseudo-polynomial DP. For the special case of networks based on SP digraphs with a unique
source and a unique sink, we provided an algorithm running in polynomial time.
For future work, we will study the complexity of the RobMCFŌēĪproblem on SP digraphs
if the number of scenarios is part of the input. Furthermore, we will study the RobMCFŌēĪ
problem for further graph classes as digraphs with bounded treewidth.
