Abstract
ThisinvestigationdealswithaN-modulessoftwarereliabilitygrowthmodel(SRGM)wherein
each module attains multiple types of faults of different severity levels. During the debugging
phase, there may arise some new faults due to assumption of imperfect debugging. The
reliability growth during the software testing phase governed by a non-homogeneous Poisson
Process (NHPP). The proposed study is devoted to analyze the fault debugging phenomenon
in all modules of software and to obtain expected number of faults observed, isolated and
removed. The software reliability and optimum total cost of the software development during
the testing phase are evaluated. The causes of fault occurrences in each module are different.
Thefaultsmayoccurwithdifferentfailureratesandfollowthenonlinearbehaviorwithrespect
to the testing time. The NHPP model incorporates the testing-effort expenditure required
for the faults debugging activities and governed by Weibull and logistic distributions which
inÔ¨āuence considerably not only the fault observation rate but also the time to isolate the faults
and the time to remove the faults from all N-modules. In addition to predict the reliability of N-
modular software, this study proposes optimal release policies for the SRGM by minimizing
the expected cost subject to budget and testing-effort constraints, while ensuring a prescribed
level of reliability and incorporating the effects of testing effort and efÔ¨Āciency. Some speciÔ¨Āc
models are also deduced by setting the appropriate parameters so as to match with the existing
models in the literature. Sensitivity results are presented through a numerical experiment to
demonstrate the validity of the analytical formulations and the computational tractability of
the model. An evolutionary algorithm, namely Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) based on
a dynamic penalty method, is employed to determine the optimal release time.

1 Introduction
The metric assessment and controlling the quality of the software before launching it are
the most critical issues confronted by the software industry. Accordingly, software relia-
bility growth models (SRGMs) play a dynamic role in predicting the quality attributes of
the software and allocating testing resources. A SRGM is a technique of software reliabil-
ity engineering, which is used to assess the software copiously, widen the analytic rank,
scheduling status and scrutinize the modiÔ¨Ācation in the reliability updation process. SRGMs
are helpful in quantifying the probability of successful software operation for an explicit
period of instance in an explicit period of surroundings. In software reliability literature, non
‚Äď homogeneous Poisson Process (NHPP) is widely used by the researchers to describe the
stochastic conduct of the fault ‚Äď counting process. It is characterized by the foundation of
a mean value function that works towards the expected several failures. The SRGMs using
NHPP have been developed under a variety of assumptions in the past four decades (Musa
et al., 1987; Pham et al., 1999; Huang and Kuo, 2002; Xiao & Dohi, 2011; Roy et al., 2014;
Song et al., 2017; Hui & Liu, 2020; Li et al., 2022; Williams et al., 2023; Bahnam et al.,
2024; Wang & Zhang, 2025).
The wide intense change has noticed in recent years in the research towards the fault
identiÔ¨Ācation and debugging activities of the modular software in the view of the testing
stage of the software development life phases that affects the reliability of the software very
signiÔ¨Ācantly. During the testing stage, each software module is tested over its requirement
analysis, interface and algorithm designs, implementation, and integration with the other
modules. The debugging activities are aimed to locate errors that cause faults that may
take place due to plenty of reasons such as imperfect debugging, error generation due to
incorrect programming, debugging time lag, environmental factors, etc. These inÔ¨āuences
may be different for individual module and hence, affects the reliability of the software. In
the literature, some of the researchers have proposed SRGMs due to very high complexity
of the faults to monitor the removal process with its subsequent identiÔ¨Ācation involving
observation and isolation (Singh et al., 2007; Yamada et al., 2000). For each of the modules,
the fault identiÔ¨Ācation and removal processes have been carried out separately and then the
total fault removal process is the sum of all the fault removal from each module which
has become a main apprehension for the software developers and users as well. In this
context, a non-homogeneous model for software reliability assessment has been proposed
by Gupta et al. (2019). They have studied a software reliability growth model (SRGM) in
distributedenvironmentbyinvolvingdelayeffectfactorinremovalsofidentifyingfaultsinthe
modules. Some authors have highlighted an optimization model for the component selection
problem in multi-modular software in the presence of redundant components to improve the
software reliability (Gupta et al., 2022). A recent study on complex modular software systems
highlights the need to determine optimal testing schedules across all modules while ensuring
the reliability requirements within resource constraints (Fang & Yeh, 2025). They introduced

a novel SRGM that incorporates the testers‚Äô learning effects and possible negligence during
the testing process.
A typical assumption of SGRMs is the imperfect debugging of detecting faults that cannot
be neglected at all for any real time software systems. Several researchers have paid atten-
tion to incorporate the feature of imperfect debugging by including the time dependent fault
contents in the software. Roy et al. (2013) proposed a SRGM in imperfect debugging environ-
ment by considering a new increasing fault content function, whereas Chatterjee and Shukla
(2016) developed SRGM with a new concept of imperfect fault dependency by introducing
some proportion of fault removal. In this context, noble research investigation on SRGM
has been carried out by Jain (2017) by involving the imperfect debugging assumption with
time dependent fault introduction rate which may change at some points of time instants. A
pioneering concept of easy-detected and difÔ¨Ācult-detected faults in SRGM have been studied
by Zhu and Pham (2018) wherein the imperfect fault removal is completed in phases. Li
(2021) have proposed software reliability growth model for fault correction process based
on artiÔ¨Ācial intelligence technology. Assuming fault detection process stochastically, Zhu
and Pham (2022) proposed generalized SRGM incorporating multiple environmental factors
such as reused modules fraction, module speciÔ¨Ācation change, etc. With the current scenar-
ios of automated testing and debugging tools, Samal and Kumar (2023) contributed SRGM
considering fault removal efÔ¨Āciency.
The testing coverage strategies play a key role in the software development life phases
that also ensure the quality of the product during test execution. Therefore, these strategies
show major inÔ¨āuence on the testing efforts which may consume a signiÔ¨Ācant number of
resources in terms of budget, equipment, tools, schedule, etc. Now-a-days, incorporating
testing efforts into SRGMs following special distribution, some research investigations have
appeared in software reliability literature (Huang et al., 2007; Kapur et al., 2004). Testing
effort function incorporating Weibull type time dependent distribution was used by Lin and
Huang (2008). In module-based software, testing effort associated with integrity of modules
enhances with the increase in the number of software modules. A different amount of testing
efforts is employed for different types of faults in their identiÔ¨Ācation and removal process.
The problem of optimizing the testing resource allocation, particularly testing-efforts during
testing and debugging phases of modular software systems has also been studied by some
investigators (Kapur et al., 2013; Kumar et al., 2017). Jain et al. (2020) discussed a SRGM
which involves time-dependent fault reduction factors and Gompertz type testing effort func-
tion. On the other hand, an artiÔ¨Ācial neural network based SRGM to model fault detection
and correction activities considering testing efforts has been discussed by Xiao et al. (2020).
Other developments of SRGMs in different testing efforts scenarios are due to Saxena et al.
(2022) and Pradhan et al. (2022).
The primary concern of cost formulation for software reliability analysts is to know an
optimal release time, which depends on many factors such as expected stage of reliabil-
ity, warranty stage, budget limit, proÔ¨Āciency of the software emergent, testing panel, etc.
A software is released on its completion on schedule time period with minimum faults. To
accommodate this task is a quite complex process for software testers. Moreover, if the soft-
ware is delayed in releasing, it might cause signiÔ¨Ācant Ô¨Ānancial instability and loss for the
manufacturer, so software production is well managed and controlled by testing and main-
tenance phases. An optimal testing time can be obtained by considering the cost of software
development and imposed constraints viz. reliability limit. The problem of optimal testing
time has been discussed by some researchers by framing appropriate cost function. Kimura
et al. (1999) suggested an approach to calculate the software development cost associated
with the warranty constraint and imperfect debugging phenomenon. When the manufacturer

with variety of errors and change points. A multi-criteria decision making technique has been

works by Kapur et al. (2022). Samal and Kumar (2024, 2025) proposed a software reliability
growth model that accounts for an imperfect fault-removal process, and also extended their
work by incorporating the fault-removal efÔ¨Āciency. They derived the optimal release time
and warranty period that minimize the development cost subject to the desired reliability.
In the present investigation, a framework is proposed to develop multi-module SRGM
wherein wide variety of faults may be contained in the modules of the software system even
there is a small or large number of modules. Therefore, the early detection and correction
of faults from the modules may be beneÔ¨Ācial to prevent many software defects. In the past
studies, the analysis on modular software for its reliability assessment assuming perfect
debugging environment has been discussed. From the testing viewpoint, a few researchers
incorporated the concepts of fault detection and removal to study the mean value functions
related with different fault-types in the modules. As evident, there has been sudden increase
in the development of SRGMs in imperfect debugging environment due to many real-time
embedded computer systems. Motivated by this vital signiÔ¨Ācance of imperfect debugging
concept, the present investigation assumes the time dependent fault content function which
may portray constant or increasing behavior of faults for the sake of imperfect debugging
environment. The consideration of fault severity levels, according to fault-types, match with
the real time problem encountered in modular software embedded systems. The proposed
study also includes the time dependent variants in testing effort. By taking objective to mini-
mize the development cost of the product subject to the reliability limit, available budget and
testing resources, optimal testing times policies for multi-module software system are estab-
lished. Table 1 summarizes the noble features incorporated in the recent relevant researches
on SRGMs and the proposed model.
To attain the proposed model‚Äôs objectives, the rest of the paper is further organized into six
sections. In Sect. 2, we describe module based SRGM along with requisite assumptions. In
Sect. 3, the mean value functions for fault debugging activities are determined. The optimal
release time problem for the cost maintenance is given in the next Sect. 4. Section 5 is devoted
to the numerical simulation. Conclusions are drawn in the last Sect. 6.
2 Multi-module based model
We present a software reliability growth model to enhance the reliability throughout the
testing stage. A multi-module software system is considered to study the different failure and
debugging characterization for each module. We also assume that the module has different
initial number of faults and unusual kinds of faults. The faults detection process in the
modules are not same and hence the reliability growth may not be the same for each module.

4 Optimal release policy
The optimal software release policy is determined by the testing time that minimizes the
total expected maintenance cost while satisfying reliability and budget constraints. Releas-
ing software too early may result in high failure intensity and maintenance cost, whereas
excessive testing leads to unnecessary development and operational expenses. Therefore,
determining the optimal stopping time for testing is crucial for ensuring software reliabil-
ity and economic feasibility. In addition, any delay in software release results in a loss of
customer satisfaction, which may cause signiÔ¨Ācant economic loss to the manufacturer. Fur-
thermore, prolonged release delay increases the expected maintenance effort required during
the operational phase. Hence, the release decision must balance testing cost, operational risk,
and customer dissatisfaction.

For the optimization purpose, we use Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) based on
dynamic penalty method (cf. Rosso et al., 2022). Under this method, we solve the constrained
optimization problem by converting all constraints into penalty terms and adding them to the
objective function. In the dynamic penalty method, if a particle violates any constraint, an
additional penalty is imposed on its cost value. The penalty increases gradually during the iter-
ations, so those particles are Ô¨Ārst allowed to explore a wide search space and later are forced
to move toward feasible solutions. This approach keeps the PSO structure simple, avoids
complicated constraint-handling techniques, and helps the algorithm efÔ¨Āciently determine
the optimal release time while satisfying reliability, budget and testing-effort constraints.

6 Conclusion
Mostoftheexistingresearchonthesoftwaredevelopmentlife-cyclesigniÔ¨Āesthatthesoftware
testing phase doesn‚Äôt get the optimal time considering budget and timelines of the project.
Eventually, when the software goes into production, the product (software) owner has to pay
huge amount to Ô¨Āx the bugs and defects encountered. Cost optimization and software release
strategies are the most desired criteria of a software product for scheduled delivery with
high quality output. If automated tools/techniques are available during testing, the testing
time becomes shorten and the software can be released earlier in the market. In this paper,
we have presented a SRGM with Weibull and logistics types testing effort functions wherein
multiple faults are present in the software modules. In practice, developer wants to detect more
faults. To accommodate this purpose, an involvement of automated techniques or consultants
shall be fruitful during the testing period. We have proposed a cost model to formulate
total expected maintenance cost projects during its life cycle; by incorporating realistic cost
elements including personnel training, overtime work, customer satisfaction loss, testing
effort, testing efÔ¨Āciency and scheduled delivery time constraint.
