Abstract
The aim of this paper is to establish a necessary and sufﬁcient condition for the non-emptiness
of the core in NTU games in partition function form, given an externality scheme f ∈Ex(N).
We extend the notion of convexity to incorporate externality effects. By introducing a new
concept of rationality, called collective rationality, we demonstrate the efﬁciency of the grand
coalition N. We also identify a sufﬁcient condition for the efﬁciency of the grand coalition
using the property of individual superadditivity.
Keywords Partition function form games · Core with externalities · Efﬁciency · Convexity

1 Introduction
A cooperative game with non-transferable utility is an assignment of a set of feasible utility
allocations to each coalition of players. The core of a cooperative game selects those utility
allocations that are robust to all possible deviations by coalitions.

The signiﬁcance of coalition formation in economic, political, and social analysis is widely
recognized. Ray (2007) gives several examples in which this framework is brought to life,
such as cartel formation, lobbies, customs unions, conﬂicts, public goods provision, political
party formation, and more.
In partition function games (PFG), the payoff of a coalition depends on the partition it
becomes part of, which is inﬂuenced by the formation of other coalitions. These external
effects of coalition formation are called externalities, their impact can be either positive or
negative, depending on the effects they generate. Cooperative game theory aims to address
two main issues simultaneously: identifying which coalitions are formed and determining
how the beneﬁts of cooperation are distributed among coalition members. In a discrete par-
tition function game, the allocation of coalitional payoffs is predeﬁned, which simpliﬁes
the analysis of coalition formation. Such games are characterized by the allocation of real
payoffs to each player in each partition, without internal transfers between coalitions, and
are therefore equivalent to NTU games (Lucas, 1974). There are many attempts to deﬁne a
modiﬁcation of the core for PFG games, and some authors analysed stability and efﬁciency
conditions in NTU games in partition function form, considering various types of externali-
ties and coalition formation rules. Yi [2003] explored the role of external effects in coalition
formation and analyzed stable coalition structures under different coalition formation rules.
Kóczy et al. (2018) offers a comprehensive analysis of partition function form games with
various applications. The signiﬁcance of convexity is well-established through Shapley’s
work Shapley (1971): he showed that the core of a convex game is not empty and that it
has a particularly regular structure. Hafalir (2007) ﬁrst attempts that while superadditivity
is an essential concept in cooperative game theory, its straightforward extension may not
guarantee the efﬁciency of the grand coalition in the presence of externalities. To address
this, the author proposes a condition akin to convexity, which serves as a sufﬁcient criterion
for ensuring the efﬁciency of the grand coalition and also leads to the non-emptiness of the
core. Chander (2014) introduced the concept of a strong-core for partition function games,
which generalizes the ordinary core. Abe and Funaki (2015) provided a necessary and sufﬁ-
cient condition for the non-emptiness of the core in partition function form games, and Abe
(2016) highlights that superadditivity alone is insufﬁcient to ensure the efﬁciency of the grand
coalition. Finally, Alonso-Meijide et al. (2021) introduced new notions of superadditivity and
convexity for games with coalitional externalities; while Alonso-Meijide et al. (2022) stud-
ied an order on the set of embedded coalitions extending the notions of superadditivity and
convexity for partition function form games. The aim of this paper is to outline some speciﬁc
conditions under which the coalition including all players achieves efﬁciency in environments
characterized by externalities. Although the concept of superadditivity traditionally justiﬁes
the formation of the grand coalition, this assumption is based on the notion that superaddi-
tivity ensures coalition efﬁciency. In this paper, the goal is to establish efﬁciency through
less stringent conditions than convexity, using a more general scheme of externalities. Exter-
nalities can be classiﬁed as positive or negative, indicating whether each coalition beneﬁts
or suffers when other coalitions join. Speciﬁcally, in the presence of positive externalities,
convexity proves to be excessively strong to ensure the efﬁciency of the grand coalition,
while superadditivity alone is sufﬁcient to achieve efﬁciency. Using the notion of individual

5 Conclusion
In NTU games, a coalition structure is deﬁned as a partition of the all-players set. The focus
is on adding structures to the game: the concept of coalitions is generalized, leading to more
intricate and dynamic structures. This approach aims to view coalitions not as static entities
but as sets where each element considers the others. Consequently, we introduce embedded
123

Annals of Operations Research (2026) 356:123–134
133
coalitions, which are more useful and powerful for achieving our goals. These embedded
coalitions exhibit characteristics such as individual superadditivity and unrestricted cooper-
ation possibilities within the coalition. Within this framework, externalities are introduced
to illustrate how these effects lead to the formation of new or larger groups of cooperating
players. Externality schemes act as maps to preserve embedded coalitions. Following this,
we demonstrate that efﬁciency within the grand coalition can be achieved; speciﬁcally, the
largest coalition cannot obtain less value than other coalitions. This leads to the introduction
of collective rationality, which has two signiﬁcant implications: ﬁrstly, when agents choose
to form a coalition, there cannot exist an alternative feasible allocation that all agents pre-
fer; secondly, the equilibrium remains non-renegotiable once an agent decides to deviate.
Thus, collective rationality, following the introduction of externality schemes, captures the
group effect and ensures stability within the coalition. Furthermore, we establish a neces-
sary and sufﬁcient condition for the non-emptiness of the core C( f ) under the externality
scheme f , leveraging the concept of f -increasing returns, f -convexity, f -generalized con-
vexity, and collective rationality. This comprehensive analysis allows us to explore further
notions of convexity and their their interrelations in straightforward cases, paving the way
to more complex and generic scenarios. Although this broad topic opens numerous avenues
for future research, we have laid a strong foundation for understanding the core dynamics
in NTU games with partition function forms. Thus, this work identiﬁes necessary and sufﬁ-
cient conditions for large coalition efﬁciency and stability in the presence of externalities. By
incorporating the concepts of individual superadditivity and collective rationality, we provide
a robust framework for analysing cooperative behaviour and coalition formation in complex
NTU games. This research not only deepens our understanding of cooperative game theory,
but also lays the groundwork for further investigation of more intricate and generalised forms
of convexity.
