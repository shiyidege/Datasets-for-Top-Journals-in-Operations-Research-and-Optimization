Appendix F:
Detailed results
Tables 8-15 give detailed results for each instance solved. The column Root gives the lower bound at the root
node of the Branch-and-Bound tree, after applying cuts and decremental state space relaxation. DualBnd
and Primal gives the final dual and primal bounds found, respectively. The computation time in seconds is
provided by the column indicated by Time, while Gap gives the optimality gap in percentage based on the
dual and primal bounds. The number of Branch-and-Bound nodes solved completely, meaning there are no
improving columns and the cut separation and decremental state space procedure are finished, is given by
the column #Nodes. This implies that a dual bound might have been found even if the reported number of
nodes is zero. The number of subset row cuts added to the RMP is given by the column #SRC.
For Tables 11-13, note that the code used by Desaulniers et al. (2016) requires integer values. Hence they
preprocess the data in the instances in a way that ensures integer values, while simultaneously ensuring that
the triangle inequality is satisfied. Our code does not have this requirement, and hence the preprocessing
steps are not performed, which may lead to slight discrepancies in the numerical results.
