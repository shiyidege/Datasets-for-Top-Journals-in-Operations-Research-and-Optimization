Abstract
The paper presents a hybrid method for the effective management of human resources when
executing production processes. The method was proposed for managing production oper-
ators in systems of relatively high complexity—with the possibility of multiple alternative
ways of designating operators to production stations. The method combines a novel approach
of assessing the effectiveness of production operators, the use of an intelligent algorithm and
the risk assessment method. The concept of effectiveness was used in the sense of a praxeo-
logical approach. Availability, performance and quality were deﬁned as its components, and
were evaluated through the prism of losses occurring in production processes. The aim of the
study is to develop a hybrid method in order to delegate production operators to tasks, not
only on the basis of a competence metric, but also on the basis of an individual effectiveness
indicator and the risk assessment of production process stages. For this purpose, a novel
method of assessing the effectiveness of the use of available resources when implementing
production processes (based on a set of quantitative indicators and taking into account the
categorization of production losses) was juxtaposed using the Tabu Search algorithm and the
FMEA method. The method enables a near-optimal solution to be generated—one in which
operators are assigned to production tasks in the most effective way possible, which in turn
minimizes both the risk of production delays and quality defects due to operator fault.

1 Introduction
Effective resource management is a very topical issue and is currently one of the biggest
challenges for manufacturing systems. An increase in effectiveness often entails costly invest-
ments in technology, an increase in employee utilization, and even a decrease in quality.
However, the improvement of effectiveness, when it is the result of the proper identiﬁcation
and elimination of production losses, is also possible without the above-mentioned con-
ditions (Antosz et al., 2020; Kocha´nska & Burduk, 2023; Trojanowska et al., 2018). The
concept of losses in the context of production efectiveness can be deﬁned as the failure of
the implemented production processes to achieve set goals, manifesting itself mainly in the
form of downtime, speed losses, and quality losses. Monitoring losses at different levels of
production is made possible by production indicators (Rathenshwar et al., 2013), for which
newer and more elaborate proposals are still being developed in scientiﬁc literature (Braglia
et al., 2018; Hsiao et al., 2022; Kocha´nska, 2022; Kocha´nska & Burduk, 2023; Oliveira et al.,
2019; Stadnicka & Antosz, 2018).
One of the most difﬁcult challenges in the context of production resource management is
human resource management (Wu et al., 2012). Moreover, in developed economies, human
resources are increasingly perceived as a key resource of an enterprise, as well as a source of
its competitive advantage (Prieto & Pilar Perez Santana, 2012). As a result, manufacturing
companies are constantly looking for methods to support effective resource management.
This can be seen, among other ways, in the increasingly improved methods of production
planning and scheduling, which are often based on artiﬁcial intelligence methods (Gao et al.,
2020; Musiał, 2023; Rosienkiewicz, 2021; Worasan et al., 2023; Yuan et al., 2021).
Productivity and quality are inﬂuenced not only by the capabilities of the machinery and
the skills of the operators butalso by organizationaldecisions. These decisions are particularly
challenging in processes involving a large number of employees and an extensive competence
matrix. Consequently, there is a need for methods that enable decisions close to the optimum
to be made quickly and relatively easily. These methods should propose the best plan for the
utilization of human resources in the execution of a given order during a speciﬁc production
shift, with the aim of maximizing productivity and quality.
Therefore, the aim of the study was to develop a method for the intelligent effectiveness
management of production operators by combining a method of assessing the effectiveness
of the use of available resources when implementing production processes (based on a set of
quantitative indicators and by taking into account the categorization of production losses).
The Tabu Search algorithm and the FMEA method were used for this purpose. The diagram
in Fig. 1 shows the plan of the main stages of the work.
Section 2 provides a comprehensive literature review. Section 2.1 summarizes the liter-
ature on production effectiveness assessment methods, identiﬁes the existing research gap
(Stage 1), and characterizes the method for assessing the effectiveness of resource utilization
during production processes (Stage 2). Section 2.2 describes the widely-used risk assessment
method, FMEA (Stage 3), while Sect. 2.3 discusses intelligent methods with a particular focus
on the Tabu Search algorithm (Stage 4). Section 3 introduces a hybrid method for the intel-
ligent effectiveness management of production operators (Stage 5), developed based on the

2 Literature review
2.1 Effectiveness assessment
The most common measure used to assess effectiveness is Overall Equipment Efectiveness
(OEE), which was developed by Nakajima (1988), and which is considered to be one of the
best metrics in both total productive maintenance (TPM) and Lean Manufacturing (Ander-
sson & Bellgran, 2015; Braglia et al., 2018; Muchiri & Pintelon, 2008). Since the time
of Nakaijama, numerous modiﬁcations of the OEE indicator have been developed (TEEP,
PEE, OLE, OAE, OPE, OTE, OE, OEEML, ORE, RE—described in more detail in (Oliveira
et al., 2019)). These modiﬁcations are also used in enterprises. The literature on the subject
also contains new proposals for effectiveness assessment methods, with modiﬁcations of the
OEE method being the leading ones (Braglia et al., 2018; De Ron & Rooda, 2006; Stad-
nicka & Antosz, 2018). The analysis of current effectiveness assessment methods carried
out in (Kocha´nska, 2022; Kocha´nska & Burduk, 2023) showed that these methods focus
mainly on assessing the effectiveness of machinery and equipment. However, according to
the literature on the subject, losses that reduce production effectiveness can be attributed to
various factors; not only those related to machinery, but also to tools, materials, methods and
human resources (Relkar & Nandurkar, 2012), which are considered a fundamental resource
of enterprises (even in the era of Industry 4.0) (Kadir et al., 2019).
The method of assessing the effectiveness of the use of available resources when imple-
menting production processes was developed in response to the research gap identiﬁed during
the literature review, which showed that there is a lack of a quantitative method for assessing
production process effectiveness. Such a quantitative method, taking into account the catego-
rization of production losses, would at the same time allow the contribution of the main areas
(production and production-related) regarding the generation of these losses to be evaluated.
The method was developed by one of the co-authors, with its main principles being presented
in (Kocha´nska & Burduk, 2023), and the details being described in Kocha´nska, 2022). The

2.2 Risk assessment
Risk is an inherent element of decision-making processes, and therefore also in production
and production-related processes such as scheduling, work organization, product develop-
ment, process supervision and the implementation of improvement activities (Burduk, 2022;
Derl˛ega et al., 2024; Kłos et al., 2022). Risk assessment is a challenge for managers, because
both underestimation and overestimation have dangerous consequences (Łapczy´nska, 2022).
Therefore, many methods have been developed to support the calculation, estimation and
analysis of the risk level, of which as many as 41 methods and techniques are presented in
the ISO/IEC 31010:2019 risk management—risk assessment techniques.
In the practice of manufacturing enterprises, the most popular is Failure Mode and Effects
Analysis (FMEA), which is mainly due to its universality and simplicity (Zhang et al., 2022).
The aim of FMEA is to eliminate, or at least reduce, the causes of potential threats, as well
as to increase their detection and reduce the severity of their effects (Almannai et al., 2008).
Despite the universality of the method, the literature on the subject distinguishes its different
types according to the criterion of the area of application: the most general—(System) Failure
Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA/ SFMEA), and more speciﬁc: design failure mode and
effectsanalysis(DFMEA),processfailuremodeandeffectanalysis(PFMEA),andmachinery
failure mode and effects analysis (MFMEA) (Anleitner, 2011; Antonelli & Stadnicka, 2019;
Degu & Moorthy, 2014). All types of FMEA methods are standardized techniques that aim
to analyze the causes and effects of defects in individual elements of the organization, and
also to plan preventive and corrective actions. The heart of FMEA analysis is the calculation
of the risk priority number (RPN) (Ouyang et al., 2022):
RP N  S × O × D
(5)
S the severity of the failure mode (1 if weak, 10 if strong), O the potential of failure
occurrence (1 if rarely, 10 if often), D the capability of failure detection (1 if easy, 10 if
difﬁcult)
Analysis of the risk level result for individual elements allows the most dangerous sources
of risk to be identiﬁed, and then for these sources of risk to be managed properly (including
the taking of additional preventive and corrective actions). In the proposed method, FMEA
analysis was used as a way to assess the risk level of individual production tasks performed
by operators in order to delegate more conﬁdent operators to tasks with higher risk.
2.3 Intelligent methods
The effective management of human resources is a difﬁcult process, not only due to the
dynamically developing market, growing customer requirements, and challenges regarding
an increasingly higher degree of production ﬂexibility, but also due to the variability of
internal human factors (such as leave, health, skills, competences, etc.) (Khanizad & Mon-
tazer, 2018). In more complicated cases, decisions made by people regarding the choice of
the best solution may, with an additional time regime, turn out to be incorrect (Łampika
et al., 2019). Therefore, intelligent methods are increasingly used to reduce human error in
decision-making processes, and to identify the optimal solution (Poskart et al., 2024). Various
intelligent methods were characterized, analyzed and compared in Musiał (2023). Heuristic
algorithms were indicated as the most effective methods in the area of challenges faced by
manufacturing companies, primarily due to the relatively short time needed to generate a
123

Annals of Operations Research
solution with sufﬁcient accuracy (close to the optimal one) (Bouajaja & Dridi, 2017; Musiał
et al., 2019b; Khanizad & Montazer, 2018; Łampika et al., 2019).
Based on a comprehensive literature review conducted by one of the co-authors in (Musiał,
2023), the Tabu Search (TS) algorithm was indicated as one of the most effective tools for
supporting decision-making processes in the context of human resources management in
the implementation of production tasks, which is mainly due to its simplicity and its ability
to generate a near-optimal solution in a very short time. The algorithm can be applied to
all problems that can be presented in the form of either a binary sequence or a sequence
of performed activities. Therefore, it has been widely used in transport (Fu et al., 2013;
Kergosien et al., 2011; Musiał et al., 2017) and production (Melo et al., 2012; Zheng et al.,
2014; Musiał et al., 2019b) issues for many years. The algorithm is based on the natural
process of searching for a solution by humans; it starts with a random or deﬁned solution,
checks neighboring solutions, and then from the best solutions repeats the procedure over
and over again. To avoid stopping at a local optimum and entering a cycle, the algorithm
generates a taboo list containing a list of moves already made in previous iterations. The
algorithm indicates the result closest to the optimum after performing a certain number of
iterations, or after satisfying a condition entered by the user (Fig. 2).
The effectiveness of a TS often depends on the speciﬁc problem it is applied to, the choice
of neighborhood structure, and the parameters deﬁning the taboo list and other aspects of the
algorithm.
3 Method development
The procedure for applying the method for the intelligent effectiveness management of pro-
duction operators combines the three methods characterized in the above subsections, and is
presented as follows (Fig. 3):
The method is based on assessing the effectiveness of the use of available resources when
implementing production processes (based on a set of quantitative indicators). From this
assessment, the procedure begins. After appointing a team and selecting the date and area
of assessment, production losses are measured, on the basis of which the effectiveness of
operators at given production stations is assessed. Both the entire production line and selected
production stations may be assessed. It is important to carry out measurements in various
combinations—in such a way as to be able to assess the operator’s effectiveness (and each of
its coefﬁcients) at each production station that he is authorized to operate. It is recommended
that the employee conducting the assessment knows the process and all the competencies
required in its area. The assessment includes the measurement of production losses, with
an evaluation sheet being used (an example assessment sheet is presented in (Kocha´nska,
2022). The results are presented in the form of effectiveness indicators and coefﬁcients
of availability, performance and quality. FMEA analysis is performed for the same area.
Independently, an optimization algorithm is developed in order to generate a solution for the
most effective assignment of operators to production stations, taking into account not only
the competence matrix, but also the risk at individual stages of the production process and the
current assessment of effectiveness (and its components) for each operator. The algorithm
ﬁrst analyzes areas with increased risk—in the developed method, an increased level of risk
applies to production stations where operations with a high severity of failure mode (8–10),
and a considerable occurrence rate (4–10) and detection rate (4–10) are carried out (such value
ranges are only a proposal of the authors and should be considered individually for each case,

depending on the industry and the needs of the company). For positions where operations
with this level of risk occur, the algorithm suggests that operators with the highest level of the
quality coefﬁcient are used (another criterion is the level of the effectiveness indicator). For
other operations, the algorithm selects employees based on the overall effectiveness indicator
result. After receiving a production order, the algorithm is updated with the availability of
operators in the planned time interval, based on which it generates a solution for a given
production order. Based on the results obtained, a plan for designating operators is developed.
It is worth adding that the method achieves the highest effectiveness when the data is updated
on an ongoing basis, e.g. by adapting the Andon system to automatically calculate indicators
based on reported losses.

4 Method veriﬁcation
Veriﬁcation of the method was carried out using the example of historical data regarding the
execution of production orders on an assembly line at a Lower Silesian household appliance
plant. This was based on the FMEA assessment performed by employees at the time. Using
data from reports generated on the basis of loss categorization collected by the Andon system,
effectiveness indicators for operators were calculated. Figure 4 shows a fragment of the data
table.
The above data was entered into the Tabu Search algorithm, which was also supplemented
with data from the competence matrix. Entering the data into the algorithm took about 10 min,
while generating the result took between 1 and 3 min using PC with processor Intel i7
and 32 GB ram. Algorithm has been written in Python programming language. During this
time, the algorithm checked about 2000 alternative ways of ﬁlling production positions with
available operators. In order to verify the method, the effectiveness result (achieved with and
without the method) for the intelligent effectiveness management of production operators
was compared (Fig. 5).
The average value of quality coefﬁcients for production stations with the above-mentioned
risk, which was generated by the algorithm, amounted to 93%. This is 3% more than the
solution that was used at that time in the company that has an average quality of 90%. The
average value of the performance coefﬁcients for the other positions was analyzed in a similar
way, which was 90 when the algorithm was applied. The result was 3.05% more than the
company’s result at the time—less than 87%.

5 Conclusions
The aim of the study was to develop a hybrid method for the intelligent effectiveness manage-
ment of production operators. The method proposed in the article combines a novel method of
assessing the effectiveness of the use of available resources when implementing production
processes. The method is based on a set of quantitative indicators and takes into account the
categorization of production losses, with the Tabu Search algorithm and the FMEA method
being used. The method enables production operators to be designated to tasks, not only
based on the competence metric, but also on the individual effectiveness assessment and risk
assessment of the stages of the production process.
The method has been veriﬁed using the example of an assembly line at a Lower Silesian
household appliance plant. The application of the method on the example of historical data
of a production company enabled a better result in terms of effectiveness and quality to

Fig. 5 Increase in OPE after applying the method
be achieved than in the case of the production order at that time, in which the decision-
making process was based only on the decisions of production managers. The average value
of the analyzed coefﬁcients (performance coefﬁcients for all production stations and the
quality coefﬁcients for the at-risk positions) for the plan generated by the algorithm was
approximately 3% higher than the average value of the coefﬁcients for the solution used by
the company at the time.
The analyzed example conﬁrmed the validity of applying the method for the intelligent
effectiveness management of production operators in systems with a relatively high degree of
complexity—with the possibility of many alternative ways of placing operators at production
stations. The application of the method makes it possible to maximize the effectiveness of
human resources with regards to executing production orders, while at the same time reducing
risk (by assigning operators with the highest values of quality coefﬁcients to operations with
increased risk).
The proposed method not only enables the operator’s production effectiveness to be deter-
mined, but also—and perhaps above all—their coefﬁcients, such as availability, performance
and quality, to be stated. Calculation of the values of individual coefﬁcients for the operator
enables the following:
• losses occurring due to the fault of a given operator, which are revealed during the analysis
of data for calculations, to be identiﬁed;
• problems in production to be visible, because it forces employees to report them in order
to remove responsibility for worse results;
• some errors in production to be avoided by having employees report seemingly minor
problems that they might otherwise try to solve using their own methods;
• better management of the training plan by identifying problems, while at the same time
increasing interest in training due to the employees’ desire to improve their results;
• adopted standards (e.g. assumed work rate or set changeover times) to be assessed by
comparing the results of different employees at a given position;
• the effectiveness of the way an employee is prepared to perform new duties to be assessed
by conducting research on various methods and different employees;

• employees to be motivated with regards to evaluation in the context of extending or termi-
nating cooperation, the bonus and salary system, and also when the evaluation is visible
to other employees.
As a follow-up study, the authors plan to examine the effectiveness of the method on the
example of real production systems, and also to expand the scope of the method by assessing
cooperation with suppliers. Additionally, it is planned to develop a program supporting the
process of collecting effectiveness data. This is because the method would achieve higher
effectiveness if the data were updated on an ongoing basis, such as by adapting an Andon
system to automatically calculate indicators based on reported losses.
