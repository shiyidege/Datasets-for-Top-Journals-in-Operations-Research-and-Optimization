Abstract
We consider a Markov decision process (MDP), whose total discounted utility is aggregated
recursively with a concave discount function that is not necessarily linear. The state and
action spaces are Borel spaces, and the utility function is nonnegative. We show that it can
be reduced to a turn-based stochastic game model with the total undiscounted utility. This
reduction result is then applied to the MDP problem with recursively aggregated utility to be
maximized or cost to be minimized.

6 Conclusion
In this paper, we considered a MDP whose total discounted utility was aggregated recursively
with a concave discount function that was not necessarily linear. As a main result, we showed
that, when the utility function was [0, ‚àû]-valued, it could be reduced to a two-player turn-
based stochastic game model with the total undiscounted utility. This reduction result was
then applied to the MDP problem with recursively aggregated utility (or say cost) to be
minimized. We mention that the corresponding maximization problem was investigated in
B√§uerle et al. (2021) without using this reduction. In this connection, the present paper also
suggests an alternative way of investigating that problem by referring to or establishing the
relevant results for the induced two-player zero-sum turn-based stochastic game.
