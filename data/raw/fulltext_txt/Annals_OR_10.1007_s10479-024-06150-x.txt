Abstract
In this study, we propose a fuzzy clustering algorithm for vectorial data, which is constructed
by extending the fuzziп¬Ғcation parameters in the q-divergence-based fuzzy c-means algo-
rithm (QFCM). The proposed algorithm, referred to as extended QFCM (eQFCM), is an
extension of both QFCM and the penalized fuzzy c-means algorithm proposed by Yang,
referred to as Y-type FCM (YFCM). eQFCM extends both the two-parameter QFCM and
YFCM algorithms to a four-parameter model. Through numerical experiments using an
artiп¬Ғcial dataset, we substantiate the theoretical discussion, and the effects of fuzziп¬Ғcation
parameter to clustering results are observed. Furthermore, the results of some numerical
experiments using real datasets are presented to demonstrate that the proposed algorithm
outperformed both QFCM and YFCM algorithms in terms of clustering accuracy.

1 Introduction
Fuzzy c-means (FCM), proposed by Bezdek (1981), is a pioneering fuzzy clustering algo-
rithm. The method used in this study is referred to as B-type FCM (BFCM) to distinguish it
from the other FCM-like algorithms. In BFCM, the fuzziп¬Ғcating is performed by relaxing the
membership range to [0, 1] and replacing the membership in the hard c-means (MacQueen
1967) with its power. This fuzziп¬Ғcation technique is referred to as B-type fuzziп¬Ғcation,
and the exponent is referred to as B-parameter. Miyamoto proposed the (Shannon) entropy-
regularized FCM (EFCM) (Miyamoto and Mukaidono 1997), and Menard proposed the
Tsallis-entropy-based FCM (TFCM) (Menard et al. 2003).
Thesealgorithmswereextendedbyintroducingaclustersizecontroller,wherethemethods
corresponding to BFCM, EFCM, and TFCM are referred to as the modiп¬Ғed BFCM (mBFCM)
(Miyamoto and Kurosawa 2004), modiп¬Ғed EFCM (mEFCM) (Ichihashi et al. 2000), and q-
divergence-based FCM (QFCM) (Miyamoto et al. 2008) in this study, respectively. In QFCM,
fuzziп¬Ғcation is performed by regularizing the mBFCM algorithm using q-divergence. This
fuzziп¬Ғcation technique is referred to as the Q-type. QFCM is reduced to mBFCM if the
regularizing parameter value is set to one, whereas it is reduced to mEFCM if the B-parameter

Penalized FCM, proposed by Yang (1993), is another variant of the fuzzy clustering
algorithm that penalizes BFCM using the logarithm of the cluster size controller. This method
is referred to as Y-type FCM (YFCM) in this study, and the fuzziп¬Ғcation technique in YFCM
is referred to as Y-type. Both the QFCM and YFCM objective functions are similar to each
other in the senses that as both have two fuzziп¬Ғcation parameters, including the B- and
regularizing parameters, and have factors of the power of membership. However, QFCM
does not become YFCM and vice versa even if their fuzziп¬Ғcation parameters are set to any
value, in contrast the fact that QFCM is reduced to mBFCM or mEFCM with a speciп¬Ғc
fuzziп¬Ғcation parameter value. QFCM and YFCM produce accurate clustering results; hence,
an algorithm that includes both yields more accurate clustering results.
In this study, we propose a novel clustering algorithm that includes QFCM and YFCM.
First, we observed the difference in the fuzziп¬Ғcation parameter effect between QFCM and
YFCM. The B-parameter in QFCM affects both the membership and cluster size controllers,
whereas the B-parameter in YFCM does not affects the cluster size controller, only the
membership. To adjust for such a difference, the QFCM objective function was extended
by splitting the B-parameter into two values, one for the membership and the other for the
cluster size controller. These extensions produces a general objective function that is reduced
to QFCM if two B-parameter values are the same, although it remains slightly different
from YFCM. This problem is solved by splitting the regularizing parameter in QFCM into
two parameters, one of which is the same for YFCM and the other for the remaining term.
Then, we propose a novel objective function with four fuzziп¬Ғcation parameters, where one
setting of fuzziп¬Ғcation parameter values produces the QFCM objective function, and another
setting produces the YFCM objective function. The proposed algorithm is referred to as an
extended QFCM (eQFCM). Through numerical experiments using an artiп¬Ғcial dataset, we
substantiate the theoretical discussion, and the effects of the fuzziп¬Ғcation parameter on the
clustering results are shown. Furthermore, the results of a numerical experiment using real
datasets are presented to demonstrate that the proposed algorithm outperforms QFCM and
YFCM in terms of clustering accuracy.
The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. In Sect. 2, we outline the symbols and
terms used throughout the article and presents conventional fuzzy clustering methods along
with their fuzzy classiп¬Ғcation functions. In Sect. 3, we delve into the fundamental ideas used
in the proposed approach and explain the methodology in detail. In Sect. 4, we present the
experimental results. Finally, Sect. 5 summarizes the п¬Ғndings and contributions of this study.

5 Conclusion
In this study, we have proposed the eQFCM algorithm, which extended both the two-
parameter QFCM and YFCM algorithms to a four-parameter model. Through numerical
experiments using an artiп¬Ғcial dataset, the theoretical results were veriп¬Ғed, and the effects of
fuzziп¬Ғcation parameter to clustering results were shown. Additionally, the proposed method
was evaluated on 21 cases derived from 19 real datasets, and the results showed a higher
clustering accuracy than the other methods.
However, several details regarding the proposed algorithm remain unclear, such as the
detailed mechanism for achieving high accuracy. In our future work, we will theoretically
and experimentally investigate the effect of each of the four fuzziп¬Ғcation parameters on the
membership values, as well as the performance of the proposed algorithm in scenarios with
numerous data points or clusters, complex distribution shapes, and signiп¬Ғcant noise levels.
Currently, the proposed algorithm has a limitation in that it requires predeп¬Ғned parameter
values. If these values are not appropriate, the clustering accuracy may decline. One possible
solution to overcome this limitation is parameter tuning utilizing a cluster validity index (Pal
and Bezdek 1995) to select adequate fuzziп¬Ғcation parameter values. The typical approach
to parameter optimization is a kind of exhaustive searching, which costs by the number
of parameters. As the proposed algorithm has four parameters which contrasts that both
QFCM and YFCM has two parameters, the parameter optimization of the proposed algorithm
costs signiп¬Ғcantly more than those of QFCM and YFCM, which is problematic. Future
studies should address this problem, e.g. by investigating lower dimensional subspace of

four dimensional parameter space of the proposed algorithm. Distinguishing the fuzziп¬Ғcation
parameter for the membership from that for the cluster size controller, applied to this study,
will be applied to other fuzzy clustering algorithm such as mBFCM and mEFCM, as another
future study. Furthermore, adopting the proposed fuzziп¬Ғcation technique will be investigated
to clustering algorithms for other data types such as time series data (Cerqueti et al. 2023),
uncertaindata(Tavakkoletal.2021),noise-includeddata(Parketal.2014),nodesinadirected
network (Yu et al. 2024), interval-valued data (Vovan et al. 2021), and tree-structured data
