Abstract
This paper studies continuous-time models for newsvendor problems with dynamic replen-
ishment, ﬁnancial hedging and Stackelberg competition. These factors are considered
simultaneously and the high-dimensional stochastic control models are established. High-
dimensional Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman (HJB) equations are derived for the value functions.
To circumvent the curse of dimensionality, a deep learning algorithm is proposed to solve the
HJB equations. A projection is introduced in the algorithm to avoid the gradient explosion
during the training phase. The deep learning algorithm is implemented for HJB equations
derived from the newsvendor models with dimensions up to six. Numerical outcomes vali-
date the algorithm’s accuracy and demonstrate that the high-dimensional stochastic control
models can successfully mitigate the risk.
Keywords Supply chain management · Newsvendor models · Stochastic control · Dynamic
replenishment · Financial hedging · Stackelberg game · Deep learning
1 Introduction
The newsvendor problem is one of the classical problems in supply chain management.
Single-period newsvendor models have been extensively studied in the literature (see e.g.,
Qin et al. (2011) and the numerous references therein.). Multi-period newsvendor models
have received growing interest in the last two decades due to the increasing demand on the
dynamic high-frequency management (see e.g., Berling (2008), Wang et al. (2010), Ni et al.

(2012), Jian et al. (2018), Chen et al. (2017) and Zhang et al. (2020)). Dynamic programming

computation is limited to the huge complexity. Continuous-time models are good alternative
candidates (see Kogan and Lou (2003), Øksendal et al. (2013)). Precisely Kogan and Lou

a manufacturing system and derive a polynomial-time combinatorial optimization algorithm

elberg game model of the newsvendor problem and use the stochastic maximum principle to
ﬁnd the explicit solutions.
Along the direction of continuous-time modeling, this paper constructs continuous-time
stochastic control models for the newsvendor problems involving several important factors
simultaneously, such as dynamic replenishment (see e.g., Øksendal et al. (2013), Chen et al.
(2017), Jian et al. (2018)), ﬁnancial hedging (see e.g., Vishal and Sridhar (2005), Chen et al.
(2007), Qing et al. (2007), Choi and Ruszczy´nski (2011), Ni et al. (2012), Heckmann et al.
(2015), Blomvall et al. (2022), Wang et al. (2022)), and Stackelberg competition (see Chen
et al. (2017)).
Compared to Øksendal et al. (2013), the retailer in our model can dynamically regulate the
replenishmentprocess withoutdelay, atthe costof a quantity-dependentadjusting fee. Indeed,
both retailers and suppliers in a coordinated supply chain have an incentive to minimize the
lead time and control the supply capacity (see Gallego and Scheller-Wolf (2000), Wei and
Gallien (2005), Wang et al. (2010), Zhao and Huchzermeier (2016), Ou and Feng (2019)
and Glock et al. (2020)). Moreover, risk management is taken into account in our model
for risk-averse retailers, which is not considered in Øksendal et al. (2013). Two most used
methods of modeling uncertainty in supply chain management are scenario analysis and
random variables. However, they lack the ﬂexibility to capture the evolution over time.
Stochastic differential equations (SDEs) can better reﬂect the market (see e.g. Jian et al.
(2018)). This paper further studies the SDE modeling for newsvendor problems. The demand
rate is parameterized as an Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process (see Jian et al. (2018)) and the
retailing price of the market is set to be an exogenous constant or a stochastic process under
geometric Brownian motion. External ﬁnancial tools are available for risk-averse retailers to
hedge the ﬂuctuations of demands and prices in the retailing market. Financial instruments
under geometric Brownian motion and those under Heston models (see Heston (1993)) are
considered respectively for the complete ﬁnancial market and incomplete ﬁnancial market.
Having these factors together makes the stochastic control models to be high-dimensional.
Consequently, the analytical solutions cannot be obtained like Øksendal et al. (2013). In this
paper, high-dimensional HJB equations are derived for the value functions. For solving the
high-dimensional HJB equations derived from the newsvendor problem, traditional numer-
ical methods require the construction of grids, resulting in the curse of dimensionality. The
deep learning algorithms are proposed to solve the high-dimensional HJB equations. Deep
learning is based on random sampling training, without the need of grids, which to some
extent solves the curse of dimensionality. Deep neural network has powerful nonlinear ﬁt-
ting ability in approximating high-dimensional functions and a strong capacity for solving
high-dimensional partial differential equations (PDEs) (Bach, 2017; E et al., 2017; Petersen
& Voigtlaender, 2018; E & Yu, 2018; Sirignano & Spiliopoulos, 2018; Raissi et al., 2018;
Beck et al., 2019; Huré et al., 2020; Zhang et al., 2020; Lu et al., 2021; Elbärchter et al.,
2021; Germain et al., 2022). However, these works focus on either linear PDEs or semilin-
ear PDEs. HJB equations as difﬁcult classes of full nonlinear PDEs have not been solved
in these papers. The difﬁculty is that the gradient explosion occurs in the training process.
In this paper, a projection technique is introduced to avoid gradient explosion during the

The remaining of this paper is arranged as follows. In Sect.2, we build the continuous-time
model for supply chain risk management with dynamic replenishment and derive HJB equa-
tions. In Sect.3, we study continuous-time dynamic replenishment with ﬁnancial hedging

solving the HJB equations. In Sect.5, we carry out numerical implementations and analyze

4.2 Implementation of the deep learning HJB algorithm
The algorithms in this paper are implemented by TensorFlow, a software library for deep
learning. First, we set an appropriate domain for real problems in previous sections. Then we
scale the inputs into [0,1] and scale the functions f and G accordingly. Next, we follow the
schedule in Sect.4.1 to deﬁne the loss function and do the stochastic gradient descent. Since
the neural network are composed of simple functions, all the derivatives can be computed
automatically by automatic differentiation 3 (AD).
The number of iterations is K = 10, 000. An iteration involves batches of size 100, 000.
Therefore, we use approximately 1 billion simulated time/space points to train the neural
network. The main equation has a higher dimension than the boundary equation. Usually the
weight for the main equation is bigger than that for the boundary equation, namely wd > wb
and wd + wb = 1. In this paper, we set wd = 0.9998, wb = 0.0002.
For the ﬁxed iteration number 10, 000, we test different network sizes, and the total losses
for different widths M and depths L are listed in Table 1. It is found that loss for the network
parameters with L = 8, M = 32 is the smallest. Other parameters of the network are
initialized by Tensorﬂow.
Since the selected tanh activation function is smooth, the loss function is globally con-
tinuous. But at the threshold points where the projection of gradient begins to occur, the
loss function is not differentiable. So in the training process of TensorFlow, the adaptive
subgradient method is automatically used to update the parameters (see Duchi et al. (2011)
and Johansson et al. (2008)). A descent step is taken for loss in T k which is the k-th random
batch of points in the training data set T :

6 Conclusions
This paper studies the continuous-time stochastic newsvendor model with ﬁnancial hedging,
dynamic replenishment and Stackelberg competition. These factors are considered simulta-
neously in this paper, which leads to a high-dimensional stochastic control problem. This
paper derives the high-dimensional HJB equations for the stochastic control problems and
develops deep learning algorithms to solve the HJB equations. To the best of our knowledge,
this is the ﬁrst paper to establish high-dimensional models for the newsvendor problems and
simulate the strategies using the deep learning algorithms. The stochastic control models of
newsvendor problems, powered by deep learning, can be extended to incorporate a variety of
factors, such as multi-item, inventory management, shortage loss, capital constraint, price-
dependent demand, and multi-source games, etc. Obviously, the framework proposed in this
study sheds light on these directions.
