Abstract
With the occurrence of a public health crisis, the demand for healthcare services increases,
which leads to an increase in the workload of hospitals. To overcome this predicament, hos-
pitals should increase the number of their medical staff. Adding new medical staff, especially
physicians, is a time-consuming process, and in such a situation, when the society is facing
a shortage of physicians, it is almost impossible. Physician scheduling can be a practical
solution to overcome this problem. Scheduling physicians without adding new physicians
increases the workload of physicians, and this may affect their productivity and the service
quality. To solve this problem, in addition to ﬁnancial incentives, non-ﬁnancial incentives
such as increasing physicians’ satisfaction should also be considered. Hence, by applying
a novel mixed-integer linear programming (MILP) model, this study conﬁgures a decision
support system for scheduling physicians by considering physicians’ satisfaction during a
public health crisis. The purpose of the proposed model is to maximize the fairness in the
distribution of workload among physicians by considering their preferences. It should be
noted that the satisfaction of physicians is considered using two indicators including equi-
table shifts distribution and physicians’ preferences. The effectiveness of the proposed MILP
model is examined using data from a hospital in Iran during the outbreak of the coronavirus
disease (COVID-19). The investigated hospital consists of 15 regular departments that are
served by 79 physicians. With the spread of COVID-19 pandemic, three departments are
added to the existing departments to serve the COVID-19 patients. Finally, the proposed
MILP model is implemented with and without considering physicians’ preferences, and the
effect of considering preferences on physician scheduling is shown.

1 Introduction
The increasing prevalence of personnel scheduling and rostering in service organizations like
higher education, healthcare, hospitality, emergency services, and transportation systems
is getting changed into a critical issue. There is a big difference between manufacturing
systems and service organizations in terms of scheduling. Manufacturing systems mainly
aim at total cost minimization whereas service systems are engaged in conﬂicting objectives
like total cost minimization and staff satisfaction maximization (Gunawan & Lau, 2013).
Healthcare industry is one of the industries that uses the scheduling problem to reduce
costs, increase employee satisfaction, and cover patients’demand (Abdalkareem et al., 2021).
Physicians are regarded to be among the most valuable resources in hospitals and are also
viewed as a bottleneck in the care providing process (Santos & Eriksson, 2014). Literature
review shows that the physician scheduling problem can be divided into three categories,
including"stafﬁng","rostering", and"re-planning"based on planning horizons (Erhard et al.,
2018). The main focus of stafﬁng problems is placed on making the strategic decision on the
needed size and composition of a workforce. There is a typical long-term planning horizon
in such planning problems (Liu et al., 2021). In this light, rostering problems are focused
tactical or operational ofﬂine task of producing concrete or generic shift rosters. Since the
planning horizon generally ranges from a week to a few months, these problems can be
referred to as mid-term (Cappanera et al., 2021). The discuss short-term adjustments of job
schedule is the main theme of re-planning problems while this class has also been referred
to as operational online (Erhard et al., 2018).
Financial or non-ﬁnancial themes constitute the major objectives of physician schedul-
ing. It is noteworthy that ﬁnancial objectives are dealt with in monetary units while the
non-ﬁnancial ones put focus on physicians and patients’ individual aspects and need more
sophisticated measures (Tohidi et al., 2019). For example, minimizing wage costs (Moosavi
et al., 2022), minimizing overtime (Zhang et al., 2020), and minimizing the use of outside
resources (Rath et al., 2017) like ﬁnancial objectives, minimizing workload (Heidari et al.,
2021), equitable shifts distribution (Güler & Geçici, 2020), and maximizing personal prefer-
ences for days off duty (Gross et al., 2018) are among the non-ﬁnancial objectives that have
been discussed in the literature.
In critical situations such as the occurrence of public health crises, the true allocation
of physicians is among the most challenging issues that the society may deal with (Arabi
et al., 2021). while the world and people may ﬁght and struggle with the spread of infectious
diseases, hospitals cope with providing medical services for the people involved in such
diseases (Govindan et al., 2020). Therefore, the workforce active in hospitals and medical
centers are imposed with a high workload. This state worsens when there are an insufﬁcient
number of physicians in these critical situations. This may lead ofﬁcials and decision-makers
to hire new and inexperienced practitioners as medical doctors to respond to patients’needs.
When a solid plan is adopted for the delivery of medical services to the public, some glimpses
of hope are injected to the blood of the society in such critical situations (Ghannam & Othman,
2023).
The current study aims at investigating physicians’needs and preferences since they are
indeed an inseparable element of the scheduling plan. Although ﬁnancial objectives have
always been considered as a trigger for stimulating medical doctors, the current study puts
an emphasis on non-ﬁnancial motivations in increasing both efﬁciency and service quality.
The highly increased work pressure that physicians may encounter in such unpredictable
situations, this research attempts to focus on physicians’well-being and job satisfaction since

it has been proven that satisﬁed physicians are more eager and willing to provide medical

When shifts distribution and physicians’preferences are integrated as indices of job satis-
faction in the whole system, a paradigm shift can come into play in this scope. The proposed
model not only assigns value to physicians’needs and preferences, but it also tries to estab-
lish a trade-off between workload and well-being. To this end, this model provides a suitable
condition for the optimization of scheduling so that it can be ascertained that physicians
are assigned to their intended shifts and departments. In this way, a sense of belonging and
engagement can be created into physicians’ whole being. In general, the purpose of this
research is to answer the following questions:
• How to develop a decision support system (DSS) with the aim of increasing the satisfaction
of physicians for scheduling physicians during a public health crisis?
• What items are included in the proposed model to consider the satisfaction of physicians?
• How to evaluate the validity of the proposed model?
In this vein, in this paper, a DSS based on a mixed-integer linear programming (MILP)
model is designed for the sake of scheduling physicians during public health challenges.
It is aimed to increase physicians’job satisfaction so that they can boost their motivation
and productivity. In this way, it can be ensured that high-quality services are offered to
patients. Physicians’ satisfaction is assessed based on two main factors, namely equitable
shift distribution and physicians’preferences. In terms of equitable shift distribution, the
presented MILP model aims at reducing deviation in the number of shifts each physician is
required to deal with during the pandemics. When it comes to physicians’preferences, their
preferences are incorporated into the model. Accordingly, physicians can select which days
they like to take off so that their ideal timetable can be taken into account in the scheduling.
Although the case study in this research pertains to the COVID-19 pandemic, the proposed
MILP model can be also applied to other pandemic situations. It can be put into use in
different health crises like man-made disasters (e.g., wars) and natural disasters (e.g., ﬂoods
and earthquakes). It is also noteworthy that healthcare systems may encounter signiﬁcant
challenges in the post-pandemic era. These problems may include insufﬁcient number of
physicians, increased patient demands, and maintenance of staff satisfaction for the sound
service quality. The developed DSS presents a scalable solution to tackle these issues by
means of optimizing physician scheduling and creating a trade-off between workload fairness
and physician preferences.
The current study ﬁlls a signiﬁcant gap in the related literature, which is the major con-
tribution of this study to the ﬁeld. Indeed, the incorporation of physician satisfaction into
optimization models is a key to approaching these challenges. This is feasible by integrating
equitable shift distribution and preference-based scheduling by implementing the model in a
public hospital in Iran. The results indicate that incorporating physicians’preferences into the
scheduling processes is of high importance, which conﬁrms the applicability and relevance of
the proposed model in dealing with both current and future challenging issues in healthcare
systems.
The remainder of this research is organized as follows. The related literature is reviewed
in Sect. 2. We present our proposed model in Sect. 3. In Sect. 4, we implement a case study
to reveal the applicability of the proposed model. Sections 5 and 6 are assigned to discussion
and conclusion, respectively.

2 Literature review
In this section, articles in the ﬁeld of physician scheduling are reviewed. Our main focus
will be on articles that have used mathematical programming tools for this purpose. In
this vein, Bowers et al. (2016) formulated a mixed-integer programming (MIP) model for
physician scheduling by considering physicians’preferences. The objective of their model
is to maximize physicians’preferences, and they used data from the department of neonatal
intensive care at the University of Tennessee Medical Center to validate their model.
A MIP model was suggested to schedule physicians with the aim of maximizing the
satisfaction of physicians by Huang et al. (2016). They have deﬁned weights for physicians
to consider equity.
An optimization model for physician scheduling in the long-term time horizon was devel-
oped by Gross (2018). Using an indicator matrix, he assigned equal shifts to physicians and
implemented his proposed model in a university hospital in Germany.
Gross et al. (2019) introduced a new concept of fairness in the physician scheduling prob-
lem. They believe that in order to achieve fairness, the preferences and workload distribution
of the previous months should be included in the scheduling process. In this regard, they
have deﬁned a satisfaction indicator for physicians, and distributed equal satisfaction among
physicians using a mathematical programming model.
In order to trade-off between work and life of physicians, an optimization model for
fair distribution of work by Damcı-Kurt et al. (2019) was presented. They evaluated the
effectiveness of their proposed model by implementing it in a medical service provider
center in the United States for staff scheduling.
With the aim of minimizing total overtime and fair workload, two MIP models for physi-
cian scheduling in large hospital ward were developed by Mansini and Zanotti (2020). They
applied the formulated models to develop a heuristic algorithm, and by comparing the results
of the heuristic algorithm and the proposed models, they showed that their proposed algorithm
has high efﬁciency and low computing time.
Camiat et al. (2021) developed a MIP model which has two levels: (i) calculating physician
productivity, (ii) allocating physician schedules based on physician productivity to emergency
department physicians in order to align physician productivity and demand without compro-
mising physician fairness. A historical analysis of data was applied to predict the demand
for physicians and estimate their productivity. Next, they decreased the difference among
demand and productivity for each physician.
A MIP model for physician scheduling in cross-hospital with the aim of maximizing
physician satisfaction and ﬁnancial proﬁt of hospital was formulated by Huang et al. (2021).
The results of the implementation of their presented model at a hospital in Taiwan indicated
an increase in physician satisfaction along with the growth of the hospital proﬁt.
Wickert et al. (2021) presented an approach for physician scheduling problem using an
integer programming model. Their model develops a fair schedule by taking into account
the equitable distribution of day and night shifts, working hours on holidays, and balance in
overtime. It should be noted that they used a heuristic algorithm to solve their problem in
large sizes.
Focusing on various constraints in the physician scheduling problem, such as fairness,
ergonomics, workload, etc., a linear programming model was suggested by Cildoz et al.
(2021). They developed heuristic algorithms to solve their problem in large dimensions, and
showed that the proposed model has a better performance compared to the manual scheduling
system.

Tohidi et al. (2021) presented a two-level robust optimization model for physician schedul-
ing in polyclinics. Their model’s objective is to minimize the total costs. They exhibited the
effectiveness of their proposed model using the data of a university health unit in Canada and
Monte Carlo simulation algorithm.
With the aim of maximizing the ﬁnancial proﬁt of hospitals, an integer programming
model for physician scheduling was developed by Lan et al. (2022), taking into account
the multiple services and physicians with diverse qualiﬁcations. They proposed a heuristic
algorithm based on variable neighborhood search in order to reach a set of high-quality
solutions in the shortest time.
An approach based on two-stage stochastic programming model for physician scheduling
with dynamic productivity levels was formulated by Zaerpour et al. (2022). Their approach
can be implemented in departments such as the emergency department where patients have
a long waiting time. Their approach is designed to reduce waiting time without adding
new physicians. The results of implementing their approach with real data showed that the
proposed scheduling approach reduces the waiting time by 13% compared to the existing
scheduling system.
Sun et al. (2023) suggested a two-stage data-derived anesthesiologist scheduling problem
to satisfy the clinical demand under uncertainty. They used a multi-objective MIP model for
this purpose. In the ﬁrst stage of their proposed model, optimal shifts are determined based
on uncertain demand. Then, in the second stage, equitable and optimal workload is allocated.
They used the epsilon constraint method to solve their multi-objective model.
An integrated approach consisting of Monte Carlo simulation, mathematical programming
model, and epsilon-constraint technique for scheduling physicians in emergency rooms was
developed by Yanmaz and Kabak (2024). The goals of their model are to minimize total costs,
create a balance in the workload assigned to physicians, and maximize job satisfaction. By
applying real data, they obtained a set of Pareto-efﬁcient solutions.
Similarly, Werghi et al. (2024) introduced an integrated framework consisting of analytical
hierarchy process (AHP) method, mathematical programming model, and goal programming
(GP) technique to schedule physicians. Their model consists of two groups of constraints,
including hard and soft constraints. They determined the importance of soft constraints by
the AHP method and used the GP method to solve their multi-objective model. Finally, they
applied data from the venereology and dermatology department in a hospital in Tunisia to
validate their model.
With the emergence of a pandemic such as H1 N1 ﬂu, COVID-19, etc., the demand for
medical services increases and this leads to an increase in the workload of the medical staff.
In such a situation, the physician scheduling problem becomes more important. The literature
review shows that the number of researchers who have studied physician scheduling during
the pandemic is a handful. In this vein, Güler and Geçici (2020) developed a decision support
system (DSS) based on MIP model for physician scheduling considering fairness during the
COVID-19 pandemic. To serve the COVID-19 patients, they set up three special departments
for COVID services and employed the existing physicians to run these departments. They
used the data of a hospital including 14 regular and three COVID departments in Turkey to
validate their proposed DSS.
An optimization model for physician scheduling in fever clinics during the COVID-19
outbreak was proposed by Liu et al. (2021). They applied queuing theory to minimize the
length of patient queues, and used a set of algorithms to solve their proposed problem.
Lan et al. (2021) presented a meta-heuristic algorithm for scheduling physicians and nurses
in mobile cabin hospitals (MCHs) during the outbreak of COVID-19. The objective of their
problem was to minimize the number of staff assigned to the MCHs during a time horizon.

A MIP model for scheduling physicians during COVID-19 was presented by Ghannam
et al. (2024), whose goal was to minimize waiting time for patients. Their model was looking
for a suitable physician assignment based on experience, skills, and immunity items. The
results showed that suitable physician assignment has a direct effect on increasing patient
satisfaction.
The literature review shows that many researchers have employed the mathematical pro-
gramming tools to solve the physician scheduling problem. They have combined various
concepts such as fairness in workload distribution, physician preferences, etc. with the
physician scheduling problem. However, another group of researchers have investigated this
problem during a public health crisis. They believe that with the spread of a pandemic like
COVID-19, the demand for hospital services will increase and the physician scheduling
problem will become more important. Hence, in this paper, for the ﬁrst time, a MILP model
is designed to conﬁgure a practical DSS for scheduling physicians by considering physi-
cians’preferences and equitable shift distribution during a public health crisis. It should be
noted that the proposed DSS is not limited to the pandemic context and can be implemented in
various public health crises such as man-made and natural disasters and even post-pandemic
era.
3 Proposed model
Physicians’scheduling in hospitals is a complex process due to conﬂicting constraints and
physician preferences. With the emergence of a public health crisis such as COVID-19,
inﬂuenza A/H2 N2, etc., hospitals and medical centers are forced to provide new services to
deal with the emerging pandemic. In this vein, some hospitals add new departments to existing
departmentstotreatpatientscausedbythepandemic.Insuchasituation,hospitalsneedasetof
specialist physicians to serve in new departments. But due to the lack of physicians, they have
to use the available physicians to serve in the departments related to the pandemic. In other
words,regularandpandemicdepartmentsshouldbemanagedwithoutchangingthenumberof
physicians and this adds to the complexity of the physicians’scheduling problem. In situation
like this, the workload of physicians increases, and decision makers usually resort to adopt
strategies based on ﬁnancial rewards to motivate physicians. However, ﬁnancial incentives
alone are not always enough drivers, and considering equitable shifts distribution and paying

attention to physicians’preferences are among the non-ﬁnancial incentives that lead to an
increase in physicians’satisfaction. Hence, this paper develops a new MILP model for the
scheduling of physicians under the outbreak of a pandemic, with the aim of equitable shifts
distribution and considering the preferences of physicians. In the following, the assumptions
of the proposed model are given in order to better understand the problem under study.
3.1 Assumptions
• Each physician is allowed to spend the maximum of one shift per day.
• Every physician should have at least one day off after each shift.
• A physician cannot serve in the regular department and the pandemic department at the
same time.
• The number of physicians required for each department is known.
• The existing physicians are used to serve in the regular and pandemic departments, and it
is not possible to employ new physicians.
• The ability to serve at pandemic departments is determined based on the expertise of
physicians.
• Every physician is allowed to choose day(s) for off before scheduling.
• Each physician should spend a minimum number of shifts at regular and pandemic depart-
ments in the time horizon.
• Positive and negative deviation from the number of shifts determined for physicians are
allowed at pandemic departments.
• Each physician is allowed to serve a maximum of a certain number of shifts in the pandemic
departments in the time horizon.

4 Case study
In this section, the efﬁciency and performance of the proposed model are evaluated using real
world data. For this purpose, the data and information of a government hospital in Tehran
are used. This hospital has 15 regular departments served by 79 physicians. Table 1 shows
the departments and the number of physicians in each department. This hospital has two
shifts including morning (8 AM- 8 PM) and night (8 PM- 8 AM). It should be noted that
some departments, such as Orthopedics, serve only in the morning shift, but some, such as
Gynecology, are active in every two shifts, as shown in Table 1. With the outbreak of the
COVID-19 pandemic, the Ministry of Health and Medical Education obliged many hospitals
to provide services for COVID-19 patients. In this regard, this hospital has set up three
departments including COVID-outpatients (P16), COVID-inpatients (P17), and COVID-ICU
(P18) to serve the COVID-19 patients. Existing physicians are applied to manage the COVID
departments, but the schedule should be such that it does not cause disruption to the regular
departments. It should be noted that service in the COVID departments is determined based
on the expertise of physicians, as shown in Table 1.
The minimum number of shifts that each physician should spend in regular and COVID
departments is reported in Table 2. In addition, according to the minimum number of des-
ignated shifts, a maximum number of shifts allowed to serve in the COVID department is
determined for each physician, as shown in Table 2.
Another important parameter of the proposed model is the minimum number of physicians
required for each department in each shift. Tables 3 and 4 show the minimum number of
physicians required in regular and COVID departments, respectively.
As mentioned, one of the features of this model is to consider physicians’preferences in
the scheduling process. For this purpose, we run the proposed model both with and without
considering physicians’preferences, and compare their results to show the sensitivity of the
proposed model to the parameters of physicians’preferences.
In this vein, ﬁrst the proposed model is run using the provided data and without con-
sidering physicians’preferences in GAMS software using CPLEX solver. Not considering
physicians’preferences means that scheduling is based on physicians’expertise and they
don’t have the authority to take days off that they don’t want to serve. In the following,
the results obtained from the implementation of the model without considering the physi-
cians’preferences are given. In Table 10 presented in Appendix A, the results of scheduling
for all physicians during a one-month (30-days) time horizon in regular and COVID depart-
ments are provided. For example, the word"HO"in the ﬁrst row and ﬁrst column of this table

5 Discussion
When it comes to DSS towards physician scheduling during a health crisis, the strong need for
the proposal of an inclusive approach other than the old traditional approaches. The inclusion
of physicians’preferences into the scheduling approach highlights the signiﬁcance of non-
ﬁnancial stimulants. When physicians are given value and credit and their ideas are taken into
account, their job satisfaction increases and, thereby, the quality and efﬁciency of their prac-
tice and performance increase. In this state, patients also receive better medical services and
a virtuous circle comes into play. The distinguishing feature of the current study compared
to prior research, especially the one conducted by Güler and Geçici (2020) is that physi-
cians’preferences are considered while formulating the scheduling process. Güler and Geçici
(2020) did not consider physicians’preferences while we valued physicians’preferences and,
thereby, provided the conditions for them to be able to arrange their own schedule. The fact
that physicians can have some sort of control over their shifts may lead to higher degrees of
motivation among them and bring increased quality of medical services at large. Moreover,
physicians can easily take leave based on their personal life and their quality of life is also pos-
itively inﬂuenced. This ﬁnding reveals the need for appreciating physicians’preferences and
paying attention to their job satisfaction so that the ﬁnal better quality of healthcare services
and efﬁciency can come out. The consideration of physicians’preferences in shift planning
assumes higher importance during public health crises. The current proposed model proved
fruitful during the COVID-19 pandemic; therefore, it can be effectively used in different pub-
lic health crises such as earthquakes, ﬂoods, and even wars. The versatility of this model and
its applicability to different critical situations gives us this assurance that medical centers and
organizations would be able to soundly opt for resource allocation and improvement of care
quality in any unpredictable situation. When such an optimized and efﬁcient system is put
into practice, physicians’dissatisfaction may get minimized and their shortages are addressed
and wonderful outputs would be produced. In this type of scheduling, the focus is not only on
equitable shift allocation but also on valuing physicians’preferences. This approach allows
for a more inclusive and human-centric scheduling method to be implemented.
The current ﬁndings may have various signiﬁcant theoretical and practical implications,
especiallyduringpublichealthcrises.Naturally,asigniﬁcantsurgeinrequestsforhospitaland
medical services occurs in the face of a natural disaster or man-made crisis and this leads to
the lack of medical staff and other problematic issues, especially in hiring physicians. in such
a context, the development of a practical physician scheduling assumes high importance.
In this study, physician scheduling is considered as an appropriate remedy to address the
shortage of human resources, especially physicians in hospitals in the face of public health
crises. When a robust scheduling is established, effective levels of stafﬁng come into play in
order to fulﬁll the enormous request for receiving medical services in such critical contexts.
This study puts a major focus on the signiﬁcance of non-ﬁnancial stimulants like increased
physicians’satisfactions. It is noteworthy that healthcare ofﬁcials and decision-makers should
beware that physician satisfaction is a key to increase productivity and efﬁciency among them
and to hinder the possible reduced quality of service delivery that may occur from time to

time in public health crises. Such non-ﬁnancial incentives greatly help with the upgrading of
ethics and ﬁnal performance among physicians.
The design and implementation of DSS on the basis of a novel MILP model shows the
strength of technology in the betterment of physician scheduling affairs in the face of crises.
The decision-making entities should take into account such scheduling approaches to boost
the quality of healthcare services, improve the life status of physicians, and serve patients in
a better manner during public health crises. Equitable shift distribution among physicians is
a main element in increasing job satisfaction of physicians in the face of epidemics. In this
regard, it is incumbent on the related ofﬁcials and policy-makers to adopt fair scheduling
plans, especially when it comes to the practical implementation of such methods in such
a way that no physicians would be dissatisﬁed with unequitable workload shared among
their colleagues. When fairness is observed, a sense of inner satisfaction is created among
physicians and, thereby, their professional performance improves and patients receive higher
quality medical care in such critical contexts.
It is noteworthy that physician scheduling is a largely relevant problem in the post-
pandemic era. Ongoing challenges, including lack of human resources, requirement for
efﬁcient resource allocation, and increased patient demand are imposed on healthcare sys-
tems throughout the world. These problematic issues are not speciﬁc to pandemics; indeed,
they also appear during natural disasters, seasonal demands, and other situations in healthcare
systems. The current model presented in this research aims at tackling these issues while con-
sidering two important criteria, namely workload fairness and physician satisfaction, which
are required for the maintenance of service quality and staff productivity in emergency situa-
tions. The ﬁndings of this study can be applied to deal with the present and future healthcare
challenges.
6 Conclusion
Thephysicianschedulingproblemisacomplexproblemwithmultipleconﬂictingconstraints,
and the use of a traditional or ineffective model to solve it, in addition to imposing huge costs
on the hospital, will lead to the dissatisfaction of physicians and patients. The occurrence of a
public health crisis, which raises the demand for medical services, increases the importance
of physician scheduling more than ever; because the hospital should provide services for the
demand caused by the crisis in addition to regular services without adding new physicians.
Therefore, it is important to conﬁgure an effective DSS for scheduling physicians during a
public health crisis. In this vein, we presented an effective DSS based on a MILP model for
physician scheduling during a public health crisis. The objective of the developed DSS is to
maximize the justice in the distribution of shifts, which leads to increasing the satisfaction
of physicians. In addition, physicians’preferences have been included in the selection of
working days to increase their satisfaction. To validate the proposed DSS, we used the data
of a public hospital in Iran during the outbreak of COVID-19. The studied hospital included 15
regular departments that were served by 79 physicians. With the outbreak of COVID-19, three
COVIDdepartmentswereaddedtotheexistingdepartments,andtheproposedDSSscheduled
79 physicians in the context of COVID-19, taking into account their preferences. The research
ﬁndings showed that the proposed model can provide a satisfactory and fair schedule for the
hospital considering physicians’preferences. Finally, the results of the sensitivity analysis
of the parameters related to physicians’preferences showed that the output of DSS strongly
depends on physicians’preferences.

Our research, like other researches, suffers from weaknesses along with its strengths, and
focusing on these weaknesses can be a path for future studies. In this research, the weight of
all physicians is considered the same. But in reality, the physician who has more experience is
expected to be more important. It is suggested to deﬁne weights for physicians based on their
experience, in the future researches. In addition, the importance of night shift and morning
shift is not the same. For fair scheduling, the weight of working in the night shift should
be considered more than the morning shift, which is suggested to be investigated in future
studies.
