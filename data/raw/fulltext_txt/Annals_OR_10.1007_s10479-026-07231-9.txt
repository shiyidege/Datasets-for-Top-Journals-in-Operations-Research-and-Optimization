Abstract
The increasing complexity of digital supply chains has heightened the need for organized
decision-support systems. These approaches are crucial for evaluating digital servitiza-
tion efforts, especially considering interdependencies and uncertainties. While the body
of literature on digital servitization is growing, existing research mostly provides descrip-
tive assessments. It is mostly lacking formal operational research frameworks that might
enhance strategic decision-making processes. This study presents a hybrid multi-criteria
decision-making (MCDM) framework that integrates DEMATEL, the Fuzzy Analytic Hier-
archy Process (Fuzzy AHP), and MARCOS to evaluate digital servitization strategies across
three essential performance dimensions: resilience, agility, and sustainability. The method-
ological framework combines bibliometric and thematic analysis to deﬁne and structure
evaluation criteria, which are subsequently examined through a three-stage modeling pro-
cess that clariﬁes causal relationships, assesses criteria weighting under uncertainty, and
establishes a compromise-based ranking of alternatives. The ﬁndings indicate a hierarchical
framework of supply chain competences, whereby resilience-oriented elements, particularly
supply chain visibility and disruption recovery capability, are identiﬁed as signiﬁcant deter-
minants of agility and sustainability outcomes. The analyzed methodology indicated that
predictive maintenance services and platform-based ecosystems surpassed other approaches.
This discovery underscores the need to use service models that are both data-driven and
proactive. This study enhances the operations research literature by establishing a thorough
decision-support framework. This approach integrates interdependencies, unpredictability,
and multi-dimensional performance evaluation into digitally servitized supply chains. The
proposed technique integrates bibliometric analysis with quantitative modeling, providing
both theoretical and practical advantages for guiding strategic decision-making in complex
operational environments.

1 Introduction
Digital technologies are changing the way modern supply chains work and are structured
at a very fast pace. Manufacturing companies are moving away from traditional product-
focused models and toward service-oriented offerings in response to growing competition
and changing customer needs (Bressanelli et al., 2024; Favoretto et al., 2022). This change is
often called "servitization." With this change, companies can use integrated product-service
systems to create more value, build stronger relationships with customers, and gain a long-
term competitive edge (Kohtamäki et al., 2020).
The rise of new digital technologies like the Internet of Things (IoT), big data analytics,
and cloud computing has sped up this change even more. This has led to the idea of digital
servitization (Favoretto et al., 2022; Kohtamäki et al., 2020). Digital servitization is the
process of adding digital features to service-based business models. This makes it possible to
create interconnected product-service ecosystems within supply chains. Digital servitization
has become a key driver of operational change by making data easier to see, allowing real-
time monitoring, and improving coordination among people in the supply chain (Camel et al.,
2025; Sala et al., 2025; Wu et al., 2025).
These changes will signiﬁcantly impact how well supply chains work, particularly in
terms of their ability to adapt, withstand challenges, and be environmentally friendly. Dig-
ital technologies help identify problems early, allow for quick adjustments to plans when
things go wrong, and improve how resources are used. Therefore, the effectiveness of digital
servitization strategies is increasingly judged by how well they improve these three impor-
tant functions together (Mao & Wang, 2024; Spadafora & Rapaccini, 2025). However, these
aspects are closely linked and often conﬂict with each other, which makes it difﬁcult to choose
the best strategic approaches.
Despite the growing interest in digital servitization, its practical use is often limited, mainly
because of its conceptual or qualitative nature. Previous studies have mostly relied on case
studies or descriptive frameworks. While these provide valuable insights into organizational
change, they don’t offer much support for structured decision-making. As a result, there’s a
noticeable lack of formal operational research (OR) methods that can model the relationships
between supply chain capabilities and help evaluate different digital servitization strategies.
From an operations research standpoint, the shift to digital servitization can be charac-
terized as a multi-criteria decision-making (MCDM) challenge, necessitating that decision-
makers assess various strategic options against interconnected and competing performance
metrics. To deal with this level of complexity, we need analytical frameworks that can show
how criteria are related, consider how uncertain expert judgments are, and give strong rank-
ings of options.
To address this deﬁciency, the present investigation formulates a hybrid, operations
research(OR)-baseddecision-supportframeworkdesignedforevaluatingdigitalservitization
strategies within supply chains. The proposed methodology integrates the Decision-Making
Trial and Evaluation Laboratory (DEMATEL) method, the Fuzzy Analytic Hierarchy Process
(Fuzzy AHP), and the Measurement of Alternatives and Ranking according to Compromise
Solution (MARCOS) method. This combination of methodology facilitates the following: (i)
the identiﬁcation of causal relationships among evaluation criteria, (ii) the determination of
their relative importance under conditions of uncertainty, and (iii) the ranking of alternative
strategies predicated on their overall system performance. Based on operational research,
the primary contribution of this study is the creation of a hybrid decision-support model.
This model accounts for uncertainty in the evaluation process, depicts the cause-and-effect

2 Research design
2.1 Research logic and study design
This study employs a systematic two-stage research framework that combines bibliometric
knowledge extraction with operational research modeling to assess the evaluation of digital
servitization strategies within supply chains (Deng & Wang, 2025; Li et al., 2024, 2025;
Martín-Peña & Sánchez-López, 2026). Methodologically, the research problem is articu-
lated as a multi-criteria decision-making (MCDM) issue, deﬁned by numerous, interrelated
evaluation criteria and conﬂicting strategic options (Lin et al., 2024; Zhao et al., 2026; Zhou
& Shao, 2025). The inherent complexity of digitally enabled supply chains—characterized
by the dynamic interaction of resilience, agility, and sustainability—demands advanced ana-
lytical methodologies that can effectively capture causal interdependencies and uncertainty
within decision-making contexts.
The preliminary stage of this investigation employs bibliometric and thematic analysis as
a structured methodology for knowledge organization. This phase transcends mere descrip-
tion; its purpose is to identify and synthesize the fundamental decision dimensions, evaluation
criteria, and strategic options inherent within the digital servitization domain (Benedettini &
Adrodegari, 2026; Kohtamäki et al., 2020; Russo & Rapaccini, 2025). Consequently, biblio-
metric analysis functions as a data-driven foundational element for the subsequent modeling
process. The second phase involves the creation of a comprehensive operational research
(OR) framework designed for the evaluation and prioritization of digital servitization strate-
gies. This framework integrates causal analysis, criteria weighting, and alternative ranking
within a cohesive analytical structure, thereby facilitating a thorough assessment of strategic
choices amidst interdependencies and uncertainties.

The integration of these two phases establishes a coherent research framework. This
framework progresses from the analysis of empirical data to the development of a decision-

comprehension of digital servitization, while simultaneously advancing hybrid MCDM

remove those that were unnecessary or had a study focus that was too wide for the research
on the impact of digital service techniques on a robust, sustainable, and agile supply chain.
After a thorough manual evaluation of the articles, it has been determined that some of
the selected sources lack sufﬁcient information about the scope and features of the issue
being investigated. This is seen by the lack of speciﬁc information provided in their titles
and keywords. The search criteria were adjusted to remove any unnecessary citations in the
selected publications. 570 scientiﬁc papers were included in the bibliometric analysis based
on the selection criteria.

Thisstudyemploysbibliometricmethodologiesasbothexploratoryandorganizationalinstru-
ments, thereby organizing the existing literature and facilitating the development of the
operational research model that follows. These methods are not implemented in isolation;
rather, they are combined with thematic and content analysis to ascertain the fundamental
concepts that underlie digital servitization in supply chains. The analytical approach utilizes
thematic synthesis and bibliometric mapping to identify emerging trends, critical research
areas, and conceptual linkages within the discipline. Content-based interpretation facilitates
the identiﬁcation of strategic frameworks and signiﬁcant patterns, while bibliometric analysis
offers a quantitative representation of the intellectual structure (Iftikhar et al., 2024; Thakur
& Jena, 2024).

and VOSviewer are implemented. A keyword co-occurrence density map was generated with

2.4 Operational research modeling of digital servitization strategies using a hybrid
DEMATEL–fuzzy AHP–MARCOS framework
Strategic decision-making has transformed into a multifaceted analytical endeavor as supply
chains increasingly incorporate digital technologies. Organizations that are transitioning to
digital servitization must evaluate a variety of operational capabilities, such as resilience,
agility, and sustainability. Traditional evaluation methods that concentrate on a single aspect
are rendered ineffective due to the complex interactions and potential conﬂicting objectives
that frequently occur in these areas. A multi-criteria decision-making (MCDM) problem can
be viewed as the evaluation of digital servitization strategies from an operational research
perspective. This problem necessitates the use of a set of related criteria to compare various
strategic options (Boix-Cots et al., 2025; Rasmussen et al., 2023).
The current investigation establishes a hybrid decision modeling framework that is based
on operational research. Its objective is to conduct a comprehensive assessment of digital
servitization strategies within supply chains to resolve this complexity. The Fuzzy Ana-
lytic Hierarchy Process (Fuzzy AHP), the Decision-Making Trial and Evaluation Laboratory
(DEMATEL), and the Measurement of Alternatives and Ranking according to Compromise
Solution (MARCOS) method are three analytical techniques that are frequently employed in
operations research. They are frequently employed in conjunction. These three components
are integrated into the proposed framework. By integrating these techniques, it enables the
modeling of cause-and-effect relationships between decision criteria, the evaluation of their
relative importance in uncertain situations, and the prioritization of strategic options based
on their overall performance (Quezada et al., 2022; Sahu et al., 2024).
This integrated analytical approach provides supply chains with a structured methodology
for delineating digital servitization strategies. Consequently, organizations can assess the
impacts of digital transformation initiatives on sustainability, agility, and resilience. The
suggested framework incorporates distinct operations research modeling perspective, thereby
facilitating a systematic comparison of diverse strategic alternatives. This contrasts with prior
research, which predominantly employed qualitative analyses or conceptual frameworks.
The methodological integration of DEMATEL, Fuzzy AHP, and MARCOS is driven by their
complementary analytical capabilities (Saha et al., 2024a; Wang et al., 2024).
The DEMATEL methodology facilitates the identiﬁcation and modeling of causal relation-
ships among evaluation criteria, thereby elucidating the structural inﬂuence patterns inherent
in digitally enabled supply chains. This approach distinguishes between primary and depen-
dent criteria, thereby aiding researchers in comprehending the interdependence and effects of
diverse operational capabilities within intricate supply chain systems. This analytical frame-
work is especially pertinent within the domain of digital servitization, given the high degree
of interdependence among capabilities such as digital visibility, responsiveness, and resource
efﬁciency (Quezada et al., 2022; Sahu et al., 2024; Sheykhan et al., 2024).
This hybrid analytical framework offers a structured methodology for the identiﬁcation of
digital servitization strategies within supply chains, thereby allowing organizations to eval-
uate the effects of digital transformation initiatives on resilience, agility, and sustainability.
The proposed framework provides a unique operations research modeling perspective, which
contrasts with the prior research that primarily relied on qualitative analyses or theoretical
discourse. Consequently, it facilitates the systematic evaluation of a diversity of strategic
alternatives.
The complementary analytical capabilities of DEMATEL, Fuzzy AHP, and MARCOS
is the driving force behind their methodological integration. The DEMATEL method is

3 Bibliometric insights and the conceptual framework of digital
servitization
This Section integrates the ﬁndings of the thematic and bibliometric analyses to demonstrate
the evolution of digital servitization in supply chains over time and its integration into the
broader intellectual framework. The analysis provides a more comprehensive perspective
that assists in the identiﬁcation of critical decision dimensions and capability structures,
rather than merely describing the literature. In the subsequent segment, the operational
research framework will be constructed from these fundamental components. The keyword
co-occurrence network (Fig. 4) demonstrates that there is a structured but evolving research
area that is founded on three interconnected pillars. Digital technologies, including cloud
computing, big data analytics, and the Internet of Things (IoT), comprise the initial pillar.
Digital servitization is enabled by these technologies (Favoretto et al., 2022; Martín-Peña
& Sánchez-López, 2026; Sklyar et al., 2019). The second pillar is comprised of servitiza-
tion and business model innovation, which represent a transition from product-centric value
creation to service-oriented value generation. The resilience, ﬂexibility, and sustainability of
the supply chain are the primary focus of the third pillar. This framework provides a critical
insight: digital servitization functions as a conceptual bridge, linking technological capabili-
ties with operational and strategic performance outcomes (Ayala et al., 2025; Benedettini &
Adrodegari, 2026; Sklyar et al., 2019; Tronvoll et al., 2020).

4 A hybrid MCDM framework for supply chain digital servitization
strategy mapping
The evaluation and prioritization of digital servitization strategies in supply chains involve
numerous and frequently conﬂicting performance objectives. To achieve digitally enabled
service transformation, organizations must simultaneously evaluate resilience, agility, and
sustainability dimensions when developing their strategic pathways. From an operational
research perspective, this issue can be rephrased as a multi-criteria decision-making (MCDM)
problem, in which a set of evaluation criteria is used to evaluate alternative digital servitization
strategies in relation to the essential capabilities necessary for digitally servitized supply
chains.
According to the thematic synthesis of the literature presented in the preceding section,
six evaluation criteria were identiﬁed to represent the resilience, agility, and sustainability
dimensions of digital servitization. This investigation proposed a hybrid DEMATEL–Fuzzy
AHP–MARCOS framework that is predicated on these criteria.
Theconceptualdeﬁnitionsoftheevaluationcriteriautilizedintheproposeddecisionmodel
are summarized in Table 2. The criteria were derived from the literature on sustainable supply
chain management, agile operations, digital servitization, and supply chain resilience.
Table 2 presents the evaluation criteria, which were organized into a hierarchical deci-
sion model to facilitate the implementation of the hybrid MCDM framework. The decision
problem is structured into three primary dimensions, which represent the strategic objectives
of digitally servitized supply chains: resilience, agility, and sustainability, as represented in
Fig. 8. Two operational criteria are further decomposed for each dimension, which reﬂect
the primary capabilities identiﬁed in literature.

capabilities that underlie it. Resilience is exempliﬁed by the ability to recover from disrup-

Sustainability is encapsulated by resource efﬁciency and lifecycle extension. Consequently,

method to ascertain their relative relative importance.

5 Discussion, contributions and implications
The proposed hybrid MCDM framework provides a solid basis for understanding the impact
of digital servitization on supply chain performance. The results it generates are as follows.
From an operational research perspective, this section summarizes the results, emphasiz-
ing their theoretical signiﬁcance, practical implications for management, and prospective
avenues for future research inquiry. Subsequently, it establishes a connection between the
analytical results and more signiﬁcant discourses concerning digitally enabled supply chain
transformation.
5.1 Theoretical contributions
The current study reﬁnes the conceptual framework of digitally enabled supply chains and
broadens the methodological toolset for their examination, thereby facilitating the conver-
gence of digital servitization and operations research (Bressanelli et al., 2024; Favoretto et al.,
2022; Mitake et al., 2025).
Speciﬁcally, this research commences with the redeﬁnition of digital servitization. The
system is perceived as a structured decision-making process that involves multiple criteria and
interconnected capabilities, rather than a straightforward sequence of technology adoption or
business model changes. It is evident from the analysis that resilience, agility, and sustainabil-
ity are not distinct concepts. As an alternative, they are a component of a system that exerts a
speciﬁc inﬂuence on one another. The empirical identiﬁcation of resilience-related capabili-
ties—including supply chain visibility and disruption recovery—as causal drivers illustrates
a hierarchical organization of capabilities, in which downstream performance dimensions
are endogenously derived from upstream structural conditions. By including it in a systems-
oriented and interdependency-conscious analytical framework, this perspective improves the
current servitization theory (Alegoz & Karaer, 2024; Thakur & Jena, 2024; Vendrell-Herrero
et al., 2017).
Additionally, this study provides a methodological advancement by developing a hybrid
multi-criteria decision-making (MCDM) framework that integrates DEMATEL, Fuzzy
AHP, and MARCOS into a uniﬁed analytical process. The proposed framework employs
a three-layer decision logic that encompasses (i) structural modeling of interdependen-
cies (DEMATEL), (ii) uncertainty-aware prioritization of criteria (Fuzzy AHP), and (iii)
compromise-based evaluation of alternatives (MARCOS). This is distinct from conventional
applications that employ these techniques in isolation. Addressing a fundamental challenge
in operations research related to modeling complex socio-technical systems, this integrated
approach enables the concurrent examination of causality, ambiguity, and performance trade-
offs (Ecer et al., 2025a; Lopes et al., 2022). This research contributes to the expanding corpus
123

Annals of Operations Research
of operations research literature that endorses hybrid and system-integrated decision-support
models.
Third, the ﬁndings provide a theoretical explanation of the mechanisms that facilitate
performance propagation in digitally servitized supply chains. The compatibility between
the Fuzzy AHP weighting scheme and the DEMATEL-derived causal structure demonstrates
the signiﬁcance of structuring criteria in decision-making. This implies that the preferences
of decision-makers and the dynamics of the system are converging. Additionally, the results
are in accordance with the MARCOS-based ranking results indicates that there are indirect
(mediated) effects, whereby enhancements in resilience result in superior sustainability and
agility outcomes. This establishes the fact that digital servitization’s effectiveness is deter-
mined by network effects between capabilities, rather than isolated criterion optimization,
expanding upon prior research.
Lastly, the study contributes to the ﬁeld of operations research by demonstrating the
transformation of bibliometric and thematic knowledge structures into formal decision mod-
els. The current investigation does not regard literary analysis as a self-contained objective.
Rather, it utilizes this methodology as a data-informed approach to develop models, thereby
integratingqualitativeknowledgeextractionwithquantitativedecisionanalytics.Thismethod
offers a structure that can be replicated to incorporate domain-speciﬁc knowledge into
operational research modeling. As a result, it improves the practical utility and theoretical
foundations of multi-criteria decision frameworks in emerging research domains. Therefore,
the current methodological approach distinguishes the proposed framework as more than a
mere methodological application, thereby enabling the development of integrated decision-
support systems in operations research.
5.2 Managerial implications
The results of this study provide signiﬁcant managerial perspectives, converting the proposed
hybrid decision framework into actionable recommendations for strategic planning within
digitally servitized supply chains. The conclusions presented in this paper are based on a
formal multi-criteria decision framework, which enhances their analytical rigor and practical
utility in contrast to conventional prescriptive approaches.
A clear rationale for managerial action is established by identifying supply chain visibility
and disruption recovery capability as causal factors. Managers should implement a strategic
investment strategy rather than distribute resources indiscriminately across all performance
categories. This entails emphasizing the capabilities that will have the most signiﬁcant impact
on the system. Consequently, investments in digital control towers, predictive analytics,
and real-time monitoring technologies are not merely operational enhancements. They are
leverage nodes within the supply chain that have the potential to enhance sustainability and
agility.
Additionally, the results suggest that service models that are distinguished by their predic-
tive capabilities and extensive data utilization are more advantageous for strategic planning,
particularly in the contexts of platform-based ecosystems and predictive maintenance. The
transition from reactive, transaction-focused service logic to anticipatory and ecosystem-
oriented strategies is represented by this shift from a decision-making perspective. By
allowing managers to evaluate performance across a variety of interconnected criteria, the
proposed framework enables a comprehensive assessment of these strategic transformations.
This reduces the likelihood that they will rely on intuitive sensations or past experiences.
Third, managers are provided with a structured approach to managing both complexity and
123

Annals of Operations Research
uncertainty by incorporating DEMATEL and Fuzzy AHP into the decision-making process.
This implies that organizations may discontinue the utilization of straightforward evaluation
models that presume criteria are independent and instead implement a system-aware decision
logic that incorporates feedback effects and trade-offs into the analysis. This is particularly
true for digital supply chains, where decisions can have a ripple effect on multiple levels of
an organization and its associates.
In addition, the MARCOS method establishes a robust benchmarking framework that
enables the comparison of a variety of strategies and both optimal and suboptimal outcomes.
This clarity in decision-making is particularly beneﬁcial when confronted with conﬂicting
objectives and limited resources, as it facilitates more effective strategic prioritization. The
results emphasize that the success of digital servitization is contingent upon the alignment
of technological investments and organizational competencies. The causal structure that has
been observed indicates that the beneﬁts of digital technologies are only apparent when they
are integrated into cohesive operational and governance frameworks. Digital transformation
should not be perceived by managers as a collection of distinct technology initiatives. As
an alternative, it ought to be perceived as a concerted endeavor to redesign systems. This
encompasses the acquisition of new skills, the integration of various processes, and the
collaboration with other departments within the organization.
In conclusion, the proposed framework is a decision-support instrument that is highly
adaptable and can be used in a variety of sectors and scenarios. By employing a system-
atic methodology for structuring, weighting, and evaluating decision criteria, the model
enables organizations to tailor digital servitization strategies to their speciﬁc operational
environments, all while maintaining analytical coherence. The framework’s practical utility
is considerably enhanced by this adaptability, which establishes it as a critical instrument for
evidence-based strategic decision-making within complex supply chain systems.
5.3 Limitations and future research directions
The proposed framework improves the analysis of digital servitization from an integrated
operationalresearchperspective;however,itisimperativetoacknowledgespeciﬁcconstraints
to establish a structured agenda for future research. Initially, the empirical implementation is
predicated on criteria from the literature and examples of how to assess them. This method
guarantees conceptual coherence; however, it restricts the generalizability of the results.
Even though this methodology is suitable for the development and validation of models,
future research should enhance the framework by integrating ﬁrm-speciﬁc data and expert
committees in real-world industrial settings. The validation of the identiﬁed causal structures
through external sources and the provision of insights into the sectoral variations in capability
conﬁgurations would be facilitated by such extensions.
As a result, the model’s analytical structure is static, which represents the relationships
among criteria at a speciﬁc temporal juncture. Nevertheless, digitally servitized supply
chains continue to operate in dynamic environments that are deﬁned by the necessity of
adapting to new circumstances, evolving technologies, and changing network conﬁgura-
tions. The temporal evolution of interdependencies and the resulting performance outcomes
could be elucidated by the integration of dynamic modeling approaches, such as system
dynamics or time-dependent MCDM frameworks, in future research endeavors. Third, the
hybrid DEMATEL–Fuzzy AHP–MARCOS framework effectively encapsulates causal rela-
tionships, uncertainty, and classiﬁcation, despite its fundamentally anchored in expert-driven
evaluation methodologies. This method is in accordance with the established practices of
123

Annals of Operations Research
multi-criteria decision-making (MCDM). Nevertheless, it introduces the potential for sub-
jective bias in the evaluation procedure. An intriguing potential area for future research is
the integration of artiﬁcial intelligence methods, such as machine learning and reinforce-
ment learning, with data-driven approaches to enhance expert evaluations and offer adaptive,
real-time decision support.
Fourthly, empirical evidence supports the structure of the current model, which prioritizes
three primary performance domains: resilience, agility, and sustainability. Nevertheless, the
increasing importance of Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) considerations, in
conjunction with ﬁnancial risk evaluations and regulatory compliance, suggests that subse-
quent iterations could be improved by incorporating additional criteria. This expansion would
more accurately reﬂect the broader decision-making context that organizations encounter.
As a result, the model would be more applicable in contexts that are characterized by pro-
gressively stricter sustainability and governance regulations if the criteria were enhanced.
In contrast, the proposed framework is based on a centralized decision-making paradigm,
which is in opposition to the decentralized and ecosystem-focused characteristics of modern
supply chains. Future research could investigate the model’s applicability in game-theoretic
frameworks or multi-actor environments, where a variety of participants, potentially with
conﬂicting objectives, engage in digital interactions. This adaptation would be a substantial
advancement in the modeling of distributed decision-making within complex socio-technical
systems, a critical area of inquiry in modern operations research.
Consequently, future research endeavors could be structured in accordance with opera-
tions research principles, which would involve the integration of multiple decision-making
divisions. The primary focus of strategic-level investigations should be the improvement
of digitally servitized supply chain ecosystems. This should include the implementation of
platform-based coordination strategies and the cultivation of enduring capabilities that are
indispensable for sustainability, agility, and resilience. Tactical-level research may focus
on the integration of supplementary performance metrics, including regulatory compliance,
ﬁnancial risk assessments, and ESG indicators, in conjunction with resource allocation
and capability prioritization. Real-time and dynamic decision-support methods could be
researched at the operational level to enhance the responsiveness and data-driven decision-
making in digitally enabled supply chains. These methodologies may encompass artiﬁcial
intelligence techniques, adaptive multi-criteria decision-making frameworks, and simulation-
based models.
