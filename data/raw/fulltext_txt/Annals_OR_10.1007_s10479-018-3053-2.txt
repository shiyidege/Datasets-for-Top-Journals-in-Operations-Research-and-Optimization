Abstract
A nonlinear programming method is used for Ô¨Ānding an equitable optimal fair division of
the unit interval [0, 1) among n players. Players‚Äô preferences are described by nonatomic
probability measures őľ1, . . . , őľn with density functions having piecewise strict monotone
likelihood ratio property. The presented algorithm can be used to obtain also an equitable
őĶ-optimal fair division in case of measures with arbitrary differentiable density functions. An
example of an equitable optimal fair division for three players is given.

Finding equitable optimal partitions for arbitrary probability measures is not easy. In the
literature of the fair division Ô¨Āeld there are known some results concerning algorithms of
Ô¨Ānding such partitions. Legut and Wilczy¬īnski (2012) showed how to obtain the optimal
partitions for two players. Dall‚ÄôAglio and Luca (2014) presented a method for Ô¨Ānding maxmin
allocations (optimal partitions) for measures satisfying three assumptions:
‚Äď complete divisibility of the good (nonatomless),
‚Äď mutual absolutely continuity,
‚Äď relative disagreement.
Under these assumptions they proposed a subgradient algorithm for obtaining the optimal
value of a fair division problem. This algorithm is based on an iterative approximation of the
solution up to a given precision and also Ô¨Ānds an almost optimal partition. In this paper we
require stronger assumptions on the measures. We assume that the density functions of the
measures satisfy strictly monotone likelihood ratio property separately on each subintervals
of some Ô¨Ānite partition of [0, 1). In our approach to obtaining equitable optimal division we
Ô¨Ānd accurate equitable optimal partitions using a nonlinear programming method.

measures deÔ¨Āned by densities being simple functions. This result was generalized by Legut
